# Warm Intro Pathfinding With AI

> Sources: Seth pasted Chris Pisarski X excerpt, 2026-06-10
> Raw: [first ingestion test](../../raw/intentional/pasted/2026-06-10-first-second-brain-ingestion-test-ai-gtm-opportunities-and-l.md)

## Overview

Seth pasted a Chris Pisarski note claiming that Claude Fable 5 is very good at finding warm intro paths for sales/GTM. The pasted excerpt says their internal benchmark/skill uses MCP-backed data while giving Claude freedom to choose filters creatively.

The reported example was a McKinsey warm-connection search: the model found people with same school, same year, same jobs, prior engagement with Chris's content, and built a full map. This page treats that as a lead from Seth's pasted raw text, not as a fully captured X source.

## Key Ideas

- Warm intro research may benefit from agent-selected filters rather than fixed CRM filters.
- Useful signals include shared school, same cohort/year, shared employers, and prior content engagement.
- A good sales/GTM agent should build a relationship map and explain why each path is warm.
- This could become a contracting offer if paired with a client's CRM, LinkedIn/X data, event lists, or other relationship graph data.

## Open Questions

- What should count as a strong versus weak warm intro path?
- What would a lightweight benchmark look like for comparing agent-selected filters against standard CRM filtering?
