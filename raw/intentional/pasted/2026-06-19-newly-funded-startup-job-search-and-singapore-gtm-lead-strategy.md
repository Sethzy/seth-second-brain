i spent a year unemployed at 22 refreshing linkedin jobs pages like it was going to save me. nobody told me the real hack: watch the money.

13 companies just raised a combined $2.5 billion this week. every single one of them is about to grow fast. new teams, new roles, new everything. and most of those roles won't be posted for weeks. by the time they hit the job boards, hundreds of people have already applied.

here's who just raised and what it means for you.

mega raises ($300M+):

[NewLimit](https://www.linkedin.com/company/newlimit/), $435M Series C (Founders Fund) - the science of aging. backed by Peter Thiel. yes, that Peter Thiel.

[Suno](https://www.linkedin.com/company/sunomusic/), $400M Series D (BOND) - AI that generates music. genuinely one of the wildest products i've seen.

[Generalist](https://www.linkedin.com/company/generalistai/), $400M (Radical Ventures) - physical AI. robots that actually understand the real world.

[TensorWave](https://www.linkedin.com/company/tensorwave/), $300M Series B (NVentures/Nvidia) - AI infrastructure. the unsexy layer that everything else runs on.

[PhysicsX](https://www.linkedin.com/company/physicsx/), $300M Series C (Temasek) - AI for physics and industrial engineering. deeply technical, deeply funded.

growth stage ($50-100M):

early stage ($7-30M):

[Sandstone](https://www.linkedin.com/company/sandstone-ai/), $30M Series A (Lightspeed) - AI for in-house legal teams. if you've ever dealt with legal paperwork you get it.

[Niteshift](https://www.linkedin.com/company/niteshiftdev/), $7M Seed (Greylock) - cloud platform for AI coding agents. tiny team, massive potential.

when i was 22 and job hunting, i had no idea funding rounds were a signal. i was just mass applying on linkedin and wondering why i never heard back. the people who were getting hired around me weren't smarter. they were paying attention to different information.

the move: find the company from this list that excites you. find someone who works there on linkedin. send them a DM that's specific and genuine. mention the raise, mention what you're interested in, ask a real question.

that's literally how i built my entire career. cold DMs to people at companies i was excited about. most didn't respond. some did. and those conversations changed everything.

save this list. come back to it when you're ready to reach out. i promise the worst thing that happens is someone doesn't reply.
