## The Template

```
{{first_name}} - if we {{Offering Valuable Service for FREE/massive discount}} for {{company_name}} to {{achieve result}} - would you be interested?

{{Signature}}

P.S. {{More Context/Social Proof}}
```

## Template Breakdown

### Opening: {{first_name}}
**Purpose:** Personalization
**Effect:** Feels less like spam
**Implementation:** Pull from CRM/database

### Core Offer: "if we {{Offering}}"
**Examples:**
- "if we audit your SEO for FREE"
- "if we build you a custom dashboard at 80% off"
- "if we optimize your cold email deliverability for FREE"

**Key:** Valuable service + FREE or massive discount

### For Whom: {{company_name}}
**Purpose:** Specificity
**Effect:** Shows you know who they are
**Implementation:** Company name from database

### Outcome: "to {{achieve result}}"
**Examples:**
- "to increase your organic traffic by 30%"
- "to save your team 10 hours per week"
- "to book 20% more qualified meetings"

**Key:** Tangible, specific result

### Call to Action: "would you be interested?"
**Not:** "Let's schedule a call"
**Not:** "Click here to learn more"
**Instead:** Low-pressure yes/no question

### Signature: {{Signature}}
**Standard:** Name, title, company
**Example:**
```
Best,
Ben Rasmussen
Founder, SalesBlast
```

### P.S.: {{More Context/Social Proof}}
**Examples:**
- "P.S. We've done this for [Company A], [Company B], and [Company C]"
- "P.S. Takes 30 minutes and you'll have a full report by EOD"
- "P.S. No strings attached - we just want to prove our value"

**Purpose:** Build credibility, reduce friction

## Why This Template Works

### 1. Value-First Approach
**Not:** "Let me tell you about our product"
**Instead:** "Let me do something valuable for you"

**Psychology:** Reciprocity - give before asking

### 2. FREE or Massive Discount
**Effect:** Removes financial objection
**Outcome:** Only objection left is time

### 3. Specific Outcome
**Not vague:** "improve your business"
**Specific:** "increase organic traffic by 30%"

**Effect:** Recipient visualizes benefit

### 4. Low-Pressure CTA
**Question, not command:** "would you be interested?"
**Easy to say yes:** Binary choice
**Easy to say no:** Not pushy

### 5. P.S. Adds Social Proof
**Placement:** P.S. gets read (proven)
**Content:** Credibility or friction reduction
**Effect:** Tips decision toward "yes"

## Template Variables Explained

### {{first_name}}
**Source:** CRM, Apollo, LinkedIn
**Format:** "John" not "John Smith"
**Effect:** Personal connection

### {{Offering Valuable Service for FREE/massive discount}}
**Examples by Industry:**
- **SaaS:** "set up a custom integration"
- **Agency:** "audit your current strategy"
- **Consulting:** "run a free workshop for your team"

**Key:** Must be genuinely valuable

### {{company_name}}
**Source:** Website, LinkedIn, database
**Format:** Official name (e.g., "Stripe" not "stripe inc")
**Effect:** Shows you researched them

### {{achieve result}}
**Format:** Specific metric + timeframe
**Examples:**
- "increase conversions by 15%"
- "reduce churn by 10%"
- "save 20 hours per month"

**Not vague:** "grow your business"

### {{More Context/Social Proof}}
**Options:**
1. Client logos: "We've done this for X, Y, Z"
2. Time commitment: "Takes 20 minutes of your time"
3. No strings: "No pitch, no upsell, just value"
4. Urgency (light): "We have 3 spots open this week"

## Key Insights

### 1. Simplicity Wins
**Template:** 3 sentences + P.S.
**Not:** Long-form pitch
**Reason:** Busy people skim, simple templates get read

### 2. Value Before Ask
**Offer:** Do something valuable for FREE
**Ask:** Only after value delivered
**Result:** Higher trust, better response rates

### 3. Specific > Vague
**Bad:** "improve your business"
**Good:** "increase organic traffic by 30%"
**Effect:** Tangible benefits easier to evaluate

### 4. Question > Command
**Bad:** "Schedule a call here"
**Good:** "would you be interested?"
**Effect:** Less pushy, easier yes

### 5. P.S. Gets Read
**Fact:** People read P.S. even if they skim body
**Strategy:** Put social proof or key detail there
**Outcome:** One last chance to convince

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Being Vague
**Bad:** "we can help you grow"
**Good:** "increase your MRR by $10K/month"

### Mistake 2: Asking for Call Immediately
**Bad:** "Let's schedule a 30-minute call"
**Good:** "would you be interested?" (call comes later)

### Mistake 3: No Social Proof
**Missing:** P.S. with credibility
**Better:** "P.S. We've done this for [Company A], [Company B]"

### Mistake 4: Offering Something Low-Value
**Bad:** "if we sent you a free PDF"
**Good:** "if we built you a custom integration"

### Mistake 5: Too Many Variables
**Bad:** {{first_name}} {{last_name}} {{title}} {{department}} {{company}}
**Good:** {{first_name}} ... {{company_name}}
**Reason:** Don't overdo personalization
