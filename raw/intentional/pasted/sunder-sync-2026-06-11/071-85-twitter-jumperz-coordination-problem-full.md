## Summary
"Biggest mistake i see with agent swarms is treating them like a technical problem when they're actually a coordination problem." Questions: Who decides which agent gets which task? How do they avoid duplicate work? How do they hand off? How does human monitor/review? Discord solved 90% of these. Coordinator spawns agents → agents work in parallel → spawn 'interns' → kill when done → everyone sees progress in channels. "Fix the coordination problem and everyone knows their roles... the whole system just flows. Maybe then you will start to enjoy scaling instead of debugging."

## Key Quote
"It's a coordination problem and discord solved almost 90% of these problems... sometimes the most boring tech wins... cause it doesn't collapse when you're just getting started."
