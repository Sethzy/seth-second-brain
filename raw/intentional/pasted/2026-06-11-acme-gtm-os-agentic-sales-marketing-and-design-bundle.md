Setting up daily scrapers with Codex and Trigger.dev to scrape custom data like government filings feels like a cheat code. Here's how we set it up for a client. 

We had a client want to scrape SEC filing information daily. They said someone on their team would manually find the information and put it into a spreadsheet.

It took just one screenshot and a prompt to Codex to get everything in the last 30 days scraped and another prompt to host it on trigger.dev to save and push straight to a Smartlead campaign. 

I feel like when I would make posts like this in the past I had to share all this detail but literally it was just 

1. give the link and a screenshot of data I want scraped. 
2. Let codex explore the structure of the site. 
3. It returned a file I validated manually that the data was correct.
4. I asked it to deploy on trigger.dev

Literally that simple.

Then we use OpenWeb Ninja and Parallel Web Systems to find the contacts based on their name and company and we're all set. 

What a time to be building systems!
