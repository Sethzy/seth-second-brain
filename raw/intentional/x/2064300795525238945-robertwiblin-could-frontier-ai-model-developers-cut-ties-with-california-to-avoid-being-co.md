## Verbatim Text

Could frontier AI model developers cut ties with California to avoid being covered by CA frontier model regulations?

OpenAI: Has an explicit agreement with the AGs office that makes that near impossible.

Alphabet: If Mountain View still controls model strategy/governance, and AI is embedded across their product suite, moving some staff or GPUs doesn't do much. Full AI-relevant exit seems wildly costly and out of character.

Anthropic: Huge California staff presence, and not really looking to go nuclear to evade regulations.

SpaceX/xAI: The most plausible case. If xAI genuinely moved all model work, governance, weights, internal use, and deployment out of CA, while only unrelated space-related activities remained, CA’s hook for regulation is significantly weakened. But any Palo Alto model work pulls it back in.

Meta: AI is integrated into everything Meta does and can't be neatly carved out and relocated elsewhere. Avoidance likely requires more than moving AI engineers: no CA model governance or decision-making, staff, internal use, or use in products offered to Californians. That’s a massive and costly shift.
