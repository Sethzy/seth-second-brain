Most reps blow the gifting follow-up.

They send a gift, then immediately email asking for a meeting. That's a quid pro quo, not gifting.

𝗛𝗲𝗿𝗲'𝘀 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗳𝗿𝗮𝗺𝗲𝘄𝗼𝗿𝗸 𝗜 𝘂𝘀𝗲 𝗶𝗻𝘀𝘁𝗲𝗮𝗱:

Subject: [gift] to [city]

Hey [First Name],

Got notified from [Amazon/Etsy/etc.] that a little mystery package should have arrived [day]... 
here's to hoping you still live on [address].

Got some intel you [reason for gift], so wanted to send over [gift].

[One line connecting the dots to why you're reaching out.]

Did you end up getting it?

-Brian

P.S. Saw you [personal observation]

Notice what's NOT in that email. No ask. No calendar link. No "would love 15 minutes."

One of my favorite responses ever: an SVP posted on LinkedIn asking "whoever sent this gorgeous piece to my office, please identify yourself so I can properly thank you." 215 likes. 

Before I even sent a follow-up. The meeting earned naturally.

Gifting process breakdown: https://t.co/Xfhujd4mUk

All on ClosedWon.

P.S. Another mistake I see - sending multiple emails, calls and then gifting. Seems desperate at that point. Make it touch #1.
