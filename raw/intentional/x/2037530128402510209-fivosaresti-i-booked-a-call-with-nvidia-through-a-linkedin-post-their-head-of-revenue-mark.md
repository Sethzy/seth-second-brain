## Verbatim Text

I booked a call with NVIDIA through a LinkedIn post.

Their Head of Revenue Marketing saw one of mine and reposted it.

I saw that signal and didn't wait for the automation to kick in.

I DM'd him directly and booked the meeting that day.

That's what I mean when I say outbound is: 

”demand capturing”

If you're only running automated sequences, you're playing 50% of the game.

The other 50% is building a system that catches first-party signals and lets your team act on them in real time.

DEMAND CREATION
• LinkedIn content that teaches your ICP something useful
• Ads that build familiarity over time
• Community presence in the spaces your buyers hang out
• Webinars and events that create real touchpoints

SIGNAL CAPTURE
• Track who engages with your posts
• Track who visits your website from LinkedIn
• Track who opens your cold emails repeatedly
• Track who attends your webinars

SIGNAL SCORING
• Score every account based on engagement depth
• Automatically update awareness stages in your CRM
• Route tier 1 accounts to reps in real time
• Flag accounts showing buying behavior for immediate follow-up

DEMAND CAPTURING
• DM the people showing buyer intent
• Call the accounts that are already aware of you
• Follow up on event attendees with personalized context
• Send manual outreach to high-signal accounts before competitors do

I track 5 awareness stages for every account.

Every account moves through them in order from identified all the way to selecting.

Each stage triggers a different action.

Automation handles the research.

Humans handle the relationship.
