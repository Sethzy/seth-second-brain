companies should never make the mistake of not tracking why they lost a sales deal

re-engaging with lost deals has been very helpful for us to add revenue, here is how:

1) always add a 2-sentence summary of the actual reason the deal died, dig deeper than just saying "budget" (timing/price/feature gap/no buy-in from a person). categorize every lost deal

2) if they went with another solution, find out who it is. I know it’s harder than it sounds, but framing it as something helpful for them (will increase the competition among their vendors and help them with negotiating new contracts) usually is enough to get even the people who are hesitant in answering this

once you do this, you will essentially just be waiting for signals to find the best time to reach out:

1) if you lost a deal because of a missing product or feature, reach out once you have it! we did the same and a lot were ready to kick start convos again since they were not happy with their providers

2) always monitor your lost accounts for new leadership hires = if someone comes new in, they usually want to leave a big impact in their first 90 days and have no loyalty to the "status quo" decisions

3) track all your champions. we saw one of our former customers joining a company that we were interested in partnering with. we reached out to him and started the convo again. this is probably one of the most undervalued advice ever. if you power a platform with 100-200 employees, a lot of them will leave for similar companies. all these people know your product and can recommend bringing you on, which you can help kick off by reaching out

4) if they didn’t go with a provider, reaching out reminding them of the cost of inaction
don’t send CTAs. always try to add value instead of "checking in after a long time". your goal is to make one of the execs forwarding your email to their team with "thoughts?". also don’t just reach out to your old contact, multi-thread the re-entry and reach out to 3-4 other relevant people
