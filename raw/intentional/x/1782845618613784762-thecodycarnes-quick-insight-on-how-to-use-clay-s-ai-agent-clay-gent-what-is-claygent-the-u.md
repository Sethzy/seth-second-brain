Quick insight on how to use Clay's AI Agent (Clay-gent)

"What is claygent?"

The use cases are endless. A simple comparison use case for this post would be Claygent vs manual SDR work to filter our 'your ICP vs Non-ICP leads'

manual SDR work = getting an account/company then looking at their website, linkedin etc and marking in your sheet "qualified/not qualified"

Claygent can do this in 1/100th of the time with 1/100th of the cost & effort.

Like @ENowoslawski says, you really need to prompt claygent/ai like a toddler. step by step, very thorough instructions. And give it the highest quality input you can. 

A strong use case example like I said is filtering out accounts that are NOT in your ICP (ideal client profile) 

E.g. once you get your raw list from
Zoominfo/apollo/listkit/other sources etc, you will almost never get your *exact* ICP. 

If you target SaaS companies exclusively, you might see a % of companies that are similar to saas, but have keywords like:

"marketing agency for saas" 

"software development" 

"saas growth" 

"custom software"

If you sent outbound to these companies thinking they are SaaS companies, you will get marked as spam.

Mass market databases will never be 100% accurate. To fix this, you can use Clay-gent or the openai integration inside of Clay to double check and see if each company is indeed either a 'SaaS company or not'

This Clay-gent 'ICP filter' is just one use case of many. 

The only input you need is: (the more input, the better.)
- company website/name
- description (optional but *highly advised*)

You can do this for

1. every outbound campaign you run.

2. inbound leads. Contact form > clay table > filter out ICP > push to email sequence or AE 

3. CRM. scrubbing existing leads. 

4. scraping competitor audience or your audience. Intent + your ICP = high positive reply rate
