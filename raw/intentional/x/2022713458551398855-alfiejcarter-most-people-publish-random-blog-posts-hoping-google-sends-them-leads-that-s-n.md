## Verbatim Text

Most people publish random blog posts hoping Google sends them leads

That's not SEO. That's gambling.

Here's how to build SEO that drives the cheapest pipeline you'll ever see

Follow these 7 steps ↓

1/ Export your money keywords

Open Google Search Console → Performance → Queries tab

Filter for keywords containing: "best", "vs", "alternative", "pricing", "review"

These are commercial intent keywords - people searching these are ready to buy, not browse

Hit Export. That spreadsheet is your pipeline blueprint

Ignore the informational fluff. Nobody ever bought software after reading "what is CRM"

2/ Pick 3-5 revenue pages and go all in

Look at your site. Find the pages that actually make money

Your pricing page. Your comparison pages. Your product landing pages

Pick the 3-5 that matter most for revenue and forget everything else

Examples: "best [category] tools" page, "[your product] vs [competitor]" page, pricing page

Most companies spread effort across 200 blog posts that generate traffic and zero pipeline

Meanwhile 3-5 pages optimised properly could generate 80% of your inbound revenue

Focus is the cheat code

3/ Build topic clusters around each page

This is where most SEO falls apart

One page alone won't rank. You need supporting content that feeds authority into it

Main page: "Best AI search tracking tools"
Supporting pages: "Best AEO tools", "Best ChatGPT visibility tools", "Best Perplexity tracking tools"

Every supporting page links back to the main page.

That's how Google knows which page matters most

Quick rule - if Google shows completely different results for 2 keywords, make them separate pages. If results overlap, consolidate into one

You're building a web of content that points Google exactly where you want it to go

That's not blogging. That's engineering a ranking system

4/ Fix the technical basics or nothing else matters

Search "site:https://t.co/3g1VqiHxXU" in Google. If your page doesn't show up, it's not indexed. Fix that first

Put your primary keyword in: title tag, H1, and first 100 words of the page

Get 30+ internal links pointing to every important page

This sounds basic because it is. But 90% of sites I audit have broken indexing, missing keywords in H1s, and important pages with 3 internal links

You can have the best content on the internet and still rank page 5 because your technical foundations are fucked

Don't skip this step

5/ Upload your company DNA to AI before writing anything

Put your company info, case studies, unique methodology, customer results into Claude Projects or ChatGPT

Now when you write content, the AI pulls from YOUR data instead of producing the same generic garbage every other company publishes

This is how you create content that actually sounds like your brand

Not "Top 10 tips for better marketing" recycled from 50 other blogs

Real insights backed by your own numbers. That's what ranks AND converts

6/ Match competitor content then beat it

Run your page through Surfer or Clearscope

Find every topic your competitors cover that you don't. Add them

Match their optimisation level first. Then go deeper

More specific examples. Real data. Better structure. Actual opinions instead of corporate filler

Re-run this every 3-6 months because rankings shift and competitors don't sit still

If you're not auditing quarterly you're losing positions without even realising it

7/ Check backlinks before wasting time on content

Pull up the top 10 Google results for your target keyword

Look at their domain rating, number of backlinks to that specific page, and compare to yours

If they have 50+ backlinks and you have 5 - stop optimising content

No amount of keyword stuffing or "better content" will outrank a page with 10x your link authority

Get the backlinks first. Then the content improvements will actually move the needle

Most people do this backwards and wonder why they're stuck on page 3 for 18 months

This is one system. It's not the only one.

But it's how you stop gambling on random blog posts and start building SEO that fills your pipeline at a fraction of the cost of paid ads

Cheapest customer acquisition channel that exists if you build it right
