# Step 1: Build the AI Commercial Talent

A strong product commercial needs a recognizable human presence.

Not a random face.

Not a generic beauty model.

Not a performer who changes every time the camera cuts.

Commercials depend on continuity. The audience needs to feel that the same person is holding the same product in the same world across every shot.

APOB AI starts with the AI Influencer Generator so creators can define an original AI talent before the ad begins. For a skincare commercial, that talent should be designed around subtle realism: believable skin, natural expression, elegant wardrobe, steady posture, and a personality that supports the product without overpowering it.

This character becomes the ad's human anchor. Every frame no longer starts from zero. It starts from the same character.

# Step 2: Generate the Hero Product Frame

After the AI commercial talent is ready, the next move is to create a hero frame.

This is where Chat to Generate comes in.

Inside APOB AI, Chat to Generate uses GPT Image 2.0 as part of the creator workflow. Instead of asking for a whole commercial at once, it helps create a controlled opening image that establishes the model, product, lighting, setting, packaging, and commercial mood.

For product ads, the hero frame matters because it gives the video model a concrete visual reference. The product label, color blocking, hand placement, skin finish, wardrobe, and camera language are already defined before motion begins.

If the hero frame is vague, the video model has to guess.

If the hero frame is clear, the commercial already has direction.

# Step 3: Turn the Hero Frame Into a Commercial Storyboard

Many creators generate one beautiful product frame and send it directly into image-to-video.

That can work, but commercials are one of the hardest formats to leave to chance.

A commercial needs product logic. It needs shot progression. It needs application detail. It needs a reason for the final confidence moment to feel earned.

A stronger approach is to use Chat to Edit after the hero frame is created.

Chat to Edit also uses GPT Image 2.0 inside APOB AI, but its job is different. Instead of making another isolated image, it turns the hero frame into a production map.

The hero frame says:

> This is the product, person, and world.

> Cut like this. Apply like this. Prove the texture like this.

The prompt is there to clarify the direction, not rewrite it.

# Step 5: Edit the Commercial Like a Creator, Not a Gambler

The first generated video should not be treated as the final answer.

It should be treated as a production pass.

After the Seedance 2.0 output, creators should review the commercial the same way a director reviews a cut:

- Does the character remain the same person in every shot?

- Does the LumaDerm package keep the same shape, label, and color blocking?

- Does the sunscreen texture look believable when applied?

- Does the skin look real, not glossy or airbrushed?

- Does the sequence build from product ritual to confidence?

- Does the final packshot work as a social thumbnail?

If something breaks, the solution is not always a longer prompt. Often the better move is to edit the storyboard, tighten the shot notes, clarify the packaging reference, or regenerate only the weak stage of the workflow.
