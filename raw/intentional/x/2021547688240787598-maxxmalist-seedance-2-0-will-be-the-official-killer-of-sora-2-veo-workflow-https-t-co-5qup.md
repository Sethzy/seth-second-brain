## getting started

1. select Seedance 2.0 as your model

2. choose your mode: Single-frame = upload first/last frame + a prompt. quick and simple
Multiframes = the real power. upload images, videos, audio clips and combine them all

## the @ system

seedance uses @ mentions to tell the model what each file is for. you upload your assets, they get labeled automatically (@Image1, @Image2, @Video1 etc), then you reference them directly in your prompt

example:

> @Image1 as the first frame, reference @Video1 for camera movement, use @Audio1 for background music

this is what gives you actual control. you're assigning roles to every single file

## what you can actually do

motion + camera replication

upload a reference video and seedance extracts:

- choreography (fight scenes, dance moves, action)

- camera techniques (dolly shots, tracking, crane, handheld)

- editing rhythm (cut timing, transitions, pacing)

- specific moves (hitchcock zooms, whip pans, orbit shots)

prompt example:

Reference @Image1 for the man's appearance in @Image2's elevator setting. Fully replicate @Video1's camera movements and the protagonist's facial expressions. Hitchcock zoom when startled, then several orbit shots inside the elevator. Doors open, tracking shot following him out.

you're stealing the cinematography from any video and applying it to your own characters and settings

character consistency

seedance 2.0 locks:

- face identity throughout the video

- product details (logos, text, fine elements)

- environment consistency across shots

- visual style (no random style drift mid-clip)

writing "@Image3 is the main character" in your prompt ensures the face stays consistent. the morphing problem is basically solved

creative template replication

find a video style you like. an ad format, a transition effect, an editing style. upload it as reference and generate new content in that exact format

this is massive for ads. find a winning creative, feed it as @Video1, swap in your product/model:

Replace the person in @Video1 with the girl in @Image1. Reference @Video1's camera work and transitions.

one-click recreation of trending content with your own characters

video extension + continuation

take an existing clip and extend it. supports smooth video extend + shot-to-shot continuity. not just "generate" but "keep filming"

set your generation duration to match how much new content you want

video editing

modify existing videos without regenerating from scratch:

- character replacement (keep the action, change the person)

- add or remove elements

- trim/cut and add segments on existing videos

- apply new visual styles

audio sync + lip sync

## pro tips

1. use high-quality references = if @Image1 is blurry, your video will be blurry. use 2K or 4K images. the AI needs clean source material

2. be explicit about references = "reference @Video1's camera movement" hits way harder than just mentioning the file

3. combine video + image tags = upload a photo as @Image1, a dance video as @Video1, prompt: "@Image1 performs the dance from @Video1." perfect motion with a custom character. this is the pro move

4. iterate small changes = don't rewrite your whole prompt if you don't like a result. change one word or swap one reference file. the speed lets you try ten versions in five minutes

5. specify edit vs reference = make clear whether you want to edit an existing video or use it as reference for something new
