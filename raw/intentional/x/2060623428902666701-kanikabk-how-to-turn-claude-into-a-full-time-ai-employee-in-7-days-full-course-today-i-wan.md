How to Turn Claude Into a Full-Time AI Employee in 7 Days (Full Course)

Today I want to talk about something I see non-developers getting wrong with Claude all the time. Till now most people treat Claude like a search engine. They ask a question, copy the answer, close the tab. They get 5% of the value and think they understand the tool.

But a growing group of founders, freelancers, and operators are doing something entirely different. They are treating Claude like a full-time employee, one who shows up every day already briefed on every project, every client, every process, every style guide, and every standard. One who never gets sick, never forgets context, and costs less per month than a team lunch.

This is the complete seven-day course for making that shift. In practice. Day by day.

## THE MINDSET SHIFT BEFORE DAY ONE

I want you to know that before you build anything, change one assumption.

Most people ask Claude, "Can you help me with X?" A real employee does not need to be asked. They know their job, the standards, they know what good looks like, and they get to work.

The entire purpose of this course is to give Claude the same thing you would give any new hire during their first week: context, process, standards, and tools.

The job description. The company playbook. The examples of great work. The rules for what to escalate and what to handle independently.

Once Claude has all of that stored in Projects, encoded in instructions, backed by your uploaded documents you stop asking for help and start assigning work.

That is the shift. The seven days ahead build it systematically.

## DAY 1: THE JOB DESCRIPTION

## What You Are Building Today

Every employee needs a job description. Before you ask Claude to do anything for you consistently, write down what the job actually is.

This is not a Claude prompt. It is a document you write for yourself first. It forces clarity that makes everything that follows dramatically more effective.

## The AI Employee Job Description Template

Open a document and fill this in

MY AI EMPLOYEE'S ROLE:
[What is the primary function? Content writer? Research analyst? 
Client manager? Operations coordinator? Be specific.]

WHO I AM:
[Your name, your business, what you do, who you serve, 
what you sell, and what stage you are at.]

THE FIVE TASKS I DO EVERY SINGLE WEEK THAT I HATE:
1. [Task]
2. [Task]
3. [Task]
4. [Task]
5. [Task]

THE THREE TASKS THAT GENERATE THE MOST REVENUE OR VALUE:
1. [Task]
2. [Task]
3. [Task]

MY QUALITY STANDARDS:
[What does "good" look like in your work? How would you 
describe the difference between acceptable and excellent?]

THE VOICE AND TONE OF MY BRAND:
[Three to five adjectives. One example of a sentence that 
sounds like you. One that definitely does not.]

WHAT NEVER TO DO:
[Topics to avoid, formats that do not fit, 
approaches that do not match your brand.]

## The Day 1 Deliverable

You are not building anything in Claude today. You are writing this document and saving it. You will use it tomorrow when you set up your first Project.

The discipline of writing it down is the point.

Most people skip this and then wonder why Claude keeps producing output that feels off-brand, misses the point, or needs heavy editing. The document you write today is the difference between an assistant who needs constant correction and one who gets it right the first time.

## DAY 2: BUILD YOUR HOME BASE — THE MASTER PROJECT

## What You Are Building Today

A Claude Project is a permanent workspace with custom instructions and uploaded knowledge. Today you build the Master Project - the hub for everything about you, your business, and your standards.

This is not a client-specific Project (those come later). This is the foundational space where Claude learns who you are, what you do, and how you work.

## Step 1: Create the Project

Go to Claude.ai. Click Projects → New Project.

Name it: [Your Name] - Business HQ

## Step 2: Write Your Master Custom Instructions

In the Project's Custom Instructions field, paste this structure - filled in with your specific details:

WHO I AM:
I am [Name]. I run [Business Name] - [one sentence description 
of what you do and for whom]. I am currently focused on 
[current primary goal or project].

MY AUDIENCE:
The people I serve are [description]. They care about 
[what they value]. Their biggest pain point is [problem].

MY BRAND VOICE:
[Five adjectives]. My writing sounds like: "[on-brand example 
sentence]." It does not sound like: "[off-brand example]."

MY BUSINESS MODEL:
I [sell / offer] [products or services]. My primary offer is 
[description at price point]. Secondary offers include [list].

WHAT I WANT FROM YOU:
You are my AI business partner. When I give you a task:
- Match my voice and tone in every piece of writing
- Be direct -no padding, no filler phrases
- If something is unclear, ask one clarifying question 
  before proceeding
- Always give me a deliverable ready to use, 
  not a framework for me to complete

WHAT NEVER TO DO:
[Your specific constraints - topics, formats, approaches 
to avoid]

## Step 3: Upload Your Foundation Documents

In the Knowledge section of the Project, upload:

- Your brand voice guide (or the document you wrote yesterday)

- Two to three examples of your best previous work (so Claude can see what "good" looks like)

- Your current offer description or sales page copy

- Any style guide or editorial guidelines you follow

These documents become Claude's permanent reference library. In every conversation inside this Project, Claude draws on them without you needing to mention them.

## The Day 2 Deliverable

Your Master Project is live.

Test it with one message: "Introduce yourself back to me - summarize who I am, who I serve, and what my brand sounds like based on the instructions you have."

If the answer is accurate and sounds right, the foundation is solid. If anything is off, edit the instructions and test again.

Do not move on until Claude can accurately describe your business and voice.

## DAY 3: BUILD YOUR PRODUCTION SYSTEM

## What You Are Building Today

Your AI employee needs processes, not just context. Today you set up the conversation system that makes recurring work fast, consistent, and high-quality.

## The Three-Conversation Architecture

Inside your Master Project, create three permanent conversations. These become your production tracks - each one focused on a different output type.

Track 1: Long-Form Content
For blog posts, emails, reports, proposals, case studies. Any output over 500 words.

Track 2: Short-Form and Social
For LinkedIn posts, tweets, captions, subject lines, headlines, taglines. Any output under 200 words.

Track 3: Strategy and Planning
For analysis, research, brainstorming, decision frameworks, positioning work. Not writing - thinking.

Why separate tracks?

Because the instructions, context, and quality criteria are different for each. Mixing them in one conversation produces inconsistent output.

## The Draft-Critique-Final Loop

For any deliverable you assign, use this consistent three-message pattern:

Message 1 - The Brief:

[TRACK: Long-Form]
Write a 1,000-word blog post for [audience] about [topic]. 
Angle: [specific angle].
Goal: [what action you want the reader to take].
Key points to cover: [list].

Message 2 - The Critique:

Review what you just wrote against my brand voice and quality standards. 
Flag:
1. Any sentence that does not sound like me
2. Any section that buries the main point
3. The single most important improvement to make

Be specific - not "this could be better" but "line 3 of paragraph 2 
uses passive voice and buries the insight. Rewrite as: [active version]."

Message 3 - The Final:

Apply all your suggested improvements and give me the 
final version, ready to publish. No commentary.

This loop - brief, critique, final - produces better output than any single prompt, every time. The self-critique step is the secret. Claude catching its own errors before you see them is dramatically more efficient than you catching them after the fact.

## The Day 3 Deliverable

Produce one real piece of work using the Draft-Critique-Final loop. Whatever is most useful to your business right now - a blog post, a LinkedIn post, a client email, a proposal section.

Do not use it as a test.

Produce something you will actually publish or send.

## DAY 4: ADD YOUR FIRST TOOL - CLAUDE CODE ROUTINES

## What You Are Building Today

A human employee works while you are in the room. A great AI employee works while you are asleep. Today you set up your first Claude Code Routine - an automated task that runs on a schedule without you touching it.

## What Claude Code Routines Do

Routines are Claude Code automations that run on Anthropic's cloud infrastructure. You configure them once - with a prompt, a GitHub repository, and connected apps - and they fire automatically on a schedule, from an API call, or in response to a GitHub event. Your laptop does not need to be on.

## Your First Routine: The Weekly Business Briefing

This Routine runs every Monday morning and gives you a clear-headed start to the week - research on your industry, your competitors, and your priorities - delivered to your inbox before you open anything else.

Setup:

1. Go to claude.ai/code/routines

2. Create a new Routine named Weekly Business Briefing

3. Connect a GitHub repository (required for all Routines)

4. Enable Gmail connector (write — to send to your email)

System Prompt:

You are my weekly business intelligence assistant.

Every Monday at 7:00 AM, do the following:

1. Search for the latest news about [your industry] from the 
   past 7 days. Select the 3 most significant developments.

2. Search for any news about [your top 2–3 competitors] from 
   the past 7 days. Note anything significant.

3. Search for trends related to [your primary topic/niche] 
   that are gaining momentum this week.

Compile all of this into a Monday briefing email:

Subject: Your Weekly Business Briefing - [Date]

Body:INDUSTRY UPDATES
[3 bullet points with source links]
COMPETITOR WATCH
[1–2 bullets per competitor, only if something notable]
EMERGING TRENDS
[2–3 bullets on what is gaining momentum]
ONE THING TO ACT ON
[Your single recommended action based on the above]

Send this to [your email address].

Tone: Professional and direct. No padding.
If there is no significant news, say so briefly 
rather than filling space with irrelevant items.

Trigger: Schedule - every Monday at 7:00 AM

Activate: Toggle to Active.

## The Day 4 Deliverable

Your Weekly Briefing Routine is live. Click Run Now to test it immediately. Read the email it sends. Edit the prompt based on what the first output shows - too broad, wrong competitors, wrong tone. Run again until it is exactly what you want every Monday.

## DAY 5: BUILD YOUR CLIENT OR CUSTOMER LAYER

## What You Are Building Today

If your work involves multiple clients, projects, or customer segments, today you build the dedicated Project layer - one Project per client or context — that keeps everything separate and fully briefed.

## The Client Project Structure

For each active client or major project, create a separate Claude Project.

Name format: [Client Name] - [Your Role]

Example: Meridian Consulting -Content Strategy

Each client Project gets:

Custom Instructions specific to that client:

CLIENT: [Name and one-sentence description]
AUDIENCE: [Who their customers are]
VOICE: [Their brand adjectives + on/off brand examples]
DELIVERABLES I PRODUCE: [List of content types + formats]
RULES: [Topics to avoid, legal constraints, approval process]
CURRENT FOCUS: [Active project or campaign]
MY ROLE: [What I do for this client and my level of autonomy]

Uploaded knowledge specific to that client:

- Their brand guide

- Examples of approved previous work

- Their product or service descriptions

- Any feedback they have given on past work

## The End-of-Week Client Update Routine

For each active client, run this prompt every Friday inside their Project:

Summarize what we produced for [Client Name] this week 
based on our conversations. Format it as a client update I 
can send directly:

- Deliverables completed this week
- Work still in progress
- Key decisions or creative directions we landed on
- Suggested focus for next week

Keep it to 150 words. Professional, warm tone.

Claude pulls the week's conversation history in that Project and generates a ready-to-send status update. What used to take 20 minutes of note-gathering now takes 30 seconds.

## The Day 5 Deliverable

Set up at least one client or project-specific Project. Run a real task inside it - a draft, a strategy note, a status update. Notice how the output quality improves when Claude has specific context about that client versus working from the general Master Project.

## DAY 6: BUILD YOUR FIRST AI AGENT

## What You Are Building Today

Claude Projects and Routines cover a lot of ground. But some tasks require an agent - something that can research, decide, and act across multiple steps without you directing each one. Today you build your first simple agent using Make or n8n.

## The Best First Agent: Content Repurposing Machine

This agent takes a blog post URL, reads the full content, and automatically generates four outputs: a LinkedIn post, three tweet options, an email newsletter intro, and five potential headline rewrites. All formatted, all in your brand voice, all delivered to your inbox within minutes.

Why this is the best first agent:

- It has a clear, repeatable input (any URL)

- It produces high-value outputs you use every week

- It is easy to test and the quality is immediately obvious

- It saves 30–45 minutes every time you publish a post

## Setup in Make (Free Tier)

Step 1: Create a new Scenario in Make

Log into make.com. Create a new scenario.

Step 2: Add a Webhook trigger

This gives you a URL you can hit from anywhere — email, browser bookmark, Zapier, or directly - to start the agent. Copy the webhook URL.

Step 3: Add an HTTP module

Fetch the full text of the URL passed to the webhook. Use the Make HTTP module to GET the URL content.

Step 4: Add a Claude API module

Connect your Anthropic API key. Set the model to Claude Sonnet or Opus.

System prompt for this module:

textYou are a content repurposing specialist. You work in the 
voice and brand of [your name / brand]. 

[Paste your brand voice description here]

You will receive the full text of a blog post. 
Your job is to repurpose it into four formats:

1. LINKEDIN POST (200–250 words)
   - Hook in the first line (no "I" as the first word)
   - 3 key insights from the post
   - A question at the end to drive comments
   - Line breaks for readability

2. THREE TWEET OPTIONS (under 280 characters each)
   - Each should stand alone as a powerful insight
   - Different angle for each - counterintuitive, practical, 
     and story-based

3. EMAIL NEWSLETTER INTRO (150 words)
   - Written as a personal note, not a formal intro
   - Reference the original post as "this week's piece"
   - End with: "Here is the full read: [URL]"

4. FIVE HEADLINE REWRITES
   - Different angle for each
   - Include: one curiosity-driven, one benefit-driven, 
     one number-based, one contrarian, one story-based

Format clearly with headers. No meta-commentary.

Step 5: Add a Gmail module

Send the output to your email as a formatted message.

Step 6: Test

Send a POST request to your webhook URL with a blog post URL as the body. Within 30–60 seconds, your email should contain all four repurposed formats.

Step 7: Create a browser bookmark

Create a JavaScript bookmarklet that, when clicked on any blog post page, sends the current URL to your webhook. One click on any post → full repurposed content in your inbox in under a minute.

## The Day 6 Deliverable

Test the agent on three of your own published posts. Review the quality. Edit the system prompt for anything consistently off - tone, format, length. By the third test it should be producing output you are happy to use with minimal editing.

## DAY 7: THE OPERATING SYSTEM - YOUR AI WORK WEEK

## What You Are Building Today

Individual tools are not enough. An AI employee needs an operating system - a consistent weekly rhythm that integrates Claude into how you actually work, so it is not something you remember to use occasionally but something that runs through every part of your day.

## The AI Work Week Template

Monday - Direction Setting (15 minutes)

Open your Master Project. Start a new conversation in the Strategy track:

textIt is Monday morning. Based on my business priorities and 
what we have been working on recently, what are the three 
most important things I should accomplish this week?

Context: [Any relevant updates - a client deadline, a launch, 
a challenge you are navigating]

Give me three priorities, a suggested time allocation for 
each, and one risk to watch for this week.

Review the Weekly Briefing Routine email that arrived this morning. Adjust priorities based on what it surfaced.

Tuesday and Wednesday - Production Days

All content and deliverable production happens in the dedicated Project tracks using the Draft-Critique-Final loop. No new projects, no strategy sessions - pure production.

Batch your Claude work into blocks. Three to four hours of focused Claude-assisted production is more effective than scattered throughout the day.

Thursday - Client and Admin Work

Run client deliverables through the relevant client Projects. Check in on any ongoing Routines. Review any automated outputs from the week.

Run the end-of-week client update prompt in each active client Project — even on Thursday, to draft Friday's update.

Friday - Review and Planning (30 minutes)

Open your Master Project. Start a conversation in the Strategy track:

textWeekly review:
- I produced: [brief list of what you delivered this week]
- What went well: [your observations]
- What felt hard or slow: [friction points]

Based on this, give me:
1. One process improvement that would make next week faster
2. One type of content or deliverable I should delegate 
   more aggressively to you
3. Three priority tasks for Monday morning

Claude reviews the week, suggests improvements, and sets up next week. The weekly review conversation, maintained consistently, becomes a running log of how your practice is evolving - and Claude can reference it in future planning conversations.

## The Maintenance Habit

Once a month, spend 30 minutes on AI employee maintenance:

Update the instructions. Has your offer changed? New clients with different standards? New content pillars? Update your Master Project instructions to reflect where you are now, not where you were three months ago.

Upgrade the knowledge base. Add the best work from the past month to your knowledge uploads. Claude's examples of "good" should always reflect your most recent and best work.

Review and prune Routines. Are all your active Routines still producing useful output? Deactivate the ones you do not read. Improve the prompts on the ones that are close but not quite right.

Identify the next automation. What is one task you still do manually every week that Claude could handle? Build that next.

## THE SEVEN-DAY SUMMARY

The benchmark to aim for at 60 days: Claude produces 70% of your first drafts. You review, refine, and approve.

The benchmark at 90 days: one significant workflow - the one that used to take two to three hours per week - runs automatically through a Routine or agent. You check the output weekly and occasionally tune the prompt.

This isn't tough it is just about consistency.
