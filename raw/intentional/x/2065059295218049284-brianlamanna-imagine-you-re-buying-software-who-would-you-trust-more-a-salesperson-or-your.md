Imagine you're buying software.

Who would you trust more?

A salesperson.

Or your close colleague?

Colleague.

Duh.

Now apply it to group presentations.

It's more powerful if you let your champion sell it, than you.

But you have to tee them up.

𝐒𝐜𝐞𝐧𝐚𝐫𝐢𝐨: 𝐊𝐚𝐫𝐞𝐧 𝐢𝐬 𝐲𝐨𝐮𝐫 𝐜𝐡𝐚𝐦𝐩𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝐚𝐧𝐝 2 𝐧𝐞𝐰 𝐩𝐞𝐨𝐩𝐥𝐞 𝐚𝐫𝐞 𝐨𝐧 𝐭𝐡𝐞 𝐜𝐚𝐥𝐥.

𝐎𝐩𝐞𝐧 𝐢𝐭 𝐥𝐢𝐤𝐞 𝐭𝐡𝐢𝐬:

"Jennifer and Steve, so great to meet you!

Karen - I'll let you open it up. Mind sharing 1) why the team is looking at ____ and 2) specifically what value you've seen thus far?"

Stay quiet. Listen attentively.

𝐃𝐮𝐫𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐭𝐡𝐞 𝐜𝐚𝐥𝐥:

Q1: “Karen - how else can you imagine the team leveraging these insights?”
Q2: “Karen - imagine we didn't exist, how would the team do this today?"
Q3: “Karen - who else on the team could see value from this?”

Tee up your champion to sell for you.

From them > from you
