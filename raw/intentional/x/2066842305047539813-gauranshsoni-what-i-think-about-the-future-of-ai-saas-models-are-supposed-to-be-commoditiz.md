What I think about the future of AI SaaS

Models are supposed to be commoditized. Harnesses are supposed to be open source. Anybody can build anything. Specialized harness architectures would be the one thing that would be, I would say, difficult to make. No, they would not be difficult.

So here is one thing: if anybody can build anything within a short amount of time, the limit is on the creativity of the harness, the creativity of the system around the model. And each system is not successful for every task.

For market research, XYZ system is usable. For sales outreach, its ABC system is a good one. For coding, we have general CLI-based tools or sandbox-based environments. So we will have different tooling around the model, and that tooling would be required. That tooling is the IP of that company.

And there is a gap in the integration of these harnesses. If I want to run marketing for a consumer business, I can rent a harness. I can use it. I'll pay for the outcome, and once my use is finished, I'll stop paying.

Creating that harness is easier, but maintaining that harness is difficult. A company like Coca-Cola, their marketing campaign is driven by AI, or their marketing team is handled by AI. And for a company like Tesla, their marketing campaigns—both will be having a shared harness for AI to work upon. But tuning that harness is the job of the provider.

And what do I mean by this? Suppose Tesla implemented an AI system for marketing for consumer sales, and Coca-Cola also implemented an AI system for marketing for consumer sales. There is some shared part which both companies will arrive at—shared learning, I would say.

And it's not about creating the harness. It's about those learnings and how quickly you can transfer those learnings from one company to another company, yet highly customize it for each company. So fundamentals will remain the same, but how you do things, how you execute, will change. Yet there is a space to fulfill.

And I believe this is where the replacement of industries will start. Consumer businesses will change. Software businesses which used to run the consumer businesses will change because consumer businesses do not change themselves.

A very small example: currently, Coca-Cola uses Adobe for its marketing campaigns. If there comes a new company which promises to deliver outcomes 2x better than Adobe, with 5x faster iterations and results at the same cost, Coca-Cola will switch. Coca-Cola will be doing better than Pepsi. Pepsi will switch to another provider for the same marketing function. They both, in fact, will switch.

Coca-Cola won't be implementing its own marketing AI solution. Instead, Coca-Cola will be providing its data, its insights, and this company needs to process them and turn them into an outcome.

And this is where the picture of SaaS will be, as I think. Not everybody can build their own software harness. Somebody will lend one to others because businesses will keep running this way. Somebody is there for someone to provide something.

But raw compute and raw harnesses are not the ones which would succeed. Otherwise, Claude Code would have been used everywhere. But Claude Code cannot be used everywhere. It is specifically designed to write code.
