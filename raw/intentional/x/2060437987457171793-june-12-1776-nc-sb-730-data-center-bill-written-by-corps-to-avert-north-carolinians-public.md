The 3 Pillars of Anti-Segmentation

When drafting an air-tight data center bill, lawmakers look at three factors to determine if separate buildings are actually one giant facility:

1st Geographical Proximity: Are the parcels touching, adjacent, or connected by private infrastructure?

2nd Corporate Ownership ("Common Control"): Are the separate LLCs ultimately answering to the same parent company or managed by the same board?

3rd Operational Interdependence: Do the facilities share backup generators, cooling loops, power substations, or data transmission lines?

If the answer to these is "yes," the state forces the megawatt loads to be aggregated (added together) against the regulatory threshold.
