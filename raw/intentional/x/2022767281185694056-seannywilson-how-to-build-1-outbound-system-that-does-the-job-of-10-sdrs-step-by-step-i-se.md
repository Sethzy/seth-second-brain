How to build 1 outbound system that does the job of 10 SDRs (step-by-step)

## I send over 1M cold emails per month across my clients. Scaled my agency from 0 to $80k/mo in 4 months. Helped a company reach 9 figures. This is every step of my outbound system, from mailbox setup to the exact email structure:

For context…

Most people can't even send 1,000 cold emails without landing in spam.

They:

1. buy a domain
2. blast a template

…and wonder why their reply rates are 0.3%.

Meanwhile, we're sending 1,000,000+ emails a month across our clients and averaging 5% reply rates.

It’s all in the infrastructure.

And I'm going to break down the entire system. From mailbox setup to the exact copy structure, so you can just steal it.

Here we go…

## 1. Build the infrastructure first (9/10 people skip this)

This is what kills 90% of cold emailers before they even start.

They send from their main domain.

One spam flag and your entire company email reputation is torched. When your CEO's emails to investors start landing in junk folders…

The game is over.

Doesn’t matter WHAT kind of targeting you did or copy you wrote.

If your infra fails, nothing else matters.

At Vertical, every one of our clients gets 50+ subdomains with 2 mailboxes each. That's 100 mailboxes per client, scattered across Google and Outlook.

Each mailbox sends 10-20 cold emails a day plus 20-30 warmup emails.

That's it.

When a subdomain gets flagged (and it will. It's a matter of when, not if), we shut it down and spin up a new one. Zero damage to the main domain.

If you want to play the volume game, you need the infrastructure to support it.

That said, you need to:

1. Authenticate everything with SPF, DMARC, and DKIM. 
2. Redirect every subdomain back to your main domain. 
3. And warm your mailboxes for a minimum of 2 weeks before sending a single cold email.

I’ll say it again, skip this step and nothing else matters.

## 2. Building your list

You could go scrape 4,000 CEOs from Apollo.

Apollo has emails for 1,800 of them.

After validation, you're left with 1,200.

Most teams just ignore the remaining 2,800 contacts. They leave 70% of their TAM on the table and wonder why pipeline is thin.

Instead of ignoring them... we run every missing contact through a waterfall that passes through 50+ data providers one by one until we find and validate their emails.

And that’s how we’re able to go from 1,200 to 3,600 usable contacts.

That's a 40% increase in TAM from enrichment alone, and it has a direct impact on pipeline.

But finding emails is only half the battle.

You need to validate everything twice. 

If you send to invalid addresses, your emails bounce. If your emails bounce, your domain reputation tanks. If your domain reputation tanks, you land in spam. And if you land in spam, you're completely cooked.

Stay under a 2% bounce rate.

Non-negotiable.

## 3. Stop blasting your entire list with the same email

This is where most "cold email agencies" completely fall apart.

They write one generic template, blast it to 10,000 people, get 12 replies and call it a success.

That's not outbound.

You need to be running different campaign types simultaneously.

These 6 are my favorite:

1. Case study campaigns: Match your social proof to lookalike companies. If you helped a fintech startup 3x their pipeline, go find every similar fintech startup and lead with that exact win.

2. Competitor campaigns: find who's using your competitor and steal them. "We noticed you're using [Competitor]. We offer double the accuracy at half the price."

3. Signal-based campaigns: if someone just got promoted, or a company just raised funding, or they're hiring for the role your product replaces… that's a great opening.

4. Website visitor campaigns: deanonymize your traffic, auto-qualify, and reach out before they forget you exist.

5. Evergreen campaigns: automate triggers like job changes, social engagement, and champion tracking. These run on autopilot.

6. Macro campaigns: your standard ICP outreach with strong copy and a lead magnet. No intent signal needed here if you have well-targeted & well-written emails.

Each campaign gets its own messaging, its own angle, and its own proof points.

One template for your entire list is lazy.

Six campaigns running simultaneously is how you scale.

## 4. Intent signals are the difference between 1% and 5% reply rates

A vast majority of cold emails fail because they can't answer one simple question:

"Why are you emailing me right now?"

If the recipient can't figure out why you're reaching out to THEM specifically, at THIS specific moment, they're hitting delete.

Intent signals give you the "why now."

- Are they hiring for a role your product replaces?

- Did they just get promoted and need to prove themselves?

- Did the company just raise a round and need to scale fast?

- Are they using a competitor you can beat?

Every signal gives you a personalized reason to reach out that feels relevant instead of random.

We find these signals in Clay, research the account, and craft messaging that speaks directly to what's happening in their world.

## 5. Write emails like a human

This is the exact cold email structure that gets us 5% reply rates:

Sentence 1: Your observation. What signal did you find? Why them, why now?

Sentence 2: Sniff a problem, solve a problem. Connect their situation to a pain you fix.

Sentence 3: Social proof. What similar companies have you helped? What were the results?

Sentence 4: Soft CTA. "Interested in learning more?". Don’t try and just book the call on your first go.

Sentence 5: P.S. line. Something personal. Statistics show P.S. lines increase response rates.

Total email: 

- Under 80 words. 
- No links. 
- No images. 
- No HTML formatting. 
- Plain text

And for follow ups… they need to add value.

DO NOT be the guy that sends: "just bumping this up" or "any thoughts?"...  

Delete it and try again. 

Reframe your pitch, highlight a different feature, send a lead magnet, etc.

Just be relevant and you’re on the right track.

## The math

100 mailboxes × 15 emails/day = 1,500 emails/day = 45,000/month per client.

Scale that across 20+ clients with proper infrastructure and you're over 1,000,000 emails/month.

At a 5% reply rate, that's 50,000 replies.

Even if only 10% of those are qualified and interested, that's 5,000 warm conversations.

The system works because every step compounds. 

Better infrastructure means better deliverability.
Better lists mean more relevant prospects. 
Better signals mean more timely outreach. 
Better copy means higher reply rates.

Miss one step and the whole thing collapses.
