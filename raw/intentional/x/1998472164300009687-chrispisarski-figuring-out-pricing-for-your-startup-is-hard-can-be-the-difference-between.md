## Verbatim Text

figuring out pricing for your startup is hard. can be the difference between double digit or triple digit growth rate at the early stage.

So we made a step by step guide based on everything we at @crustdata learned at our time during YC:

step 1: overcome the fear of asking for high numbers + understand that the process is experimental

step 2: avoid "Cost Plus" pricing (calculating your costs and adding a small margin), will just guarantee underpricing

step 3: Schedule a meeting specifically with your "Champion" (the person who wants to buy).

Step 4: be specific about what your product delivers, even if in a follow up (Cost Savings, Time Savings, or Revenue Increase).

Step 5: Calculate the specific $ amount of that value (e.g., "We save 20% of 100 agents' time" -> $100k salary x 100 agents = $10M cost -> $2M savings) and ask the champion to challenge your numbers to make sure the doc is solid  enough to show the CFO

Step 6: Set your price: Pick a number that is 25% to 50% (roughly 1/3) of the total value you calculated.

Step 7: Avoid offering long free trial and instead sign an annual contract immediately with a 30 or 60-day money-back guarantee (opt-out clause)

Step 8: aim for Gross Margin ~ 80-90%

Step 9: If your margin is low, raise the price or exit the deal (unless you care about market share).

Step 10 (and maybe most important: Don't try to match the lower price of competitors (it becomes a race to the bottom) and differentiate your product instead (functionality, specific integrations, …).

other tips:

KEEP IT SIMPLE (complexity kills deals).
 Prioritize ARR/MRR over pure usage-based pricing to protect against downturns.

don’t pretend to be a big company, play to your strength, this is the reason many YC founders offer the "Founder's personal cell phone number" and 24/7 support.

If you have absolutely no idea where to start, pick a competitor's price as a baseline and for  every new customer you speak to, increase the price by 50% until you lose 25% of deals solely because of price.

And finally stop looking back thinking about leaving money on the table, just get the logos and move on.
