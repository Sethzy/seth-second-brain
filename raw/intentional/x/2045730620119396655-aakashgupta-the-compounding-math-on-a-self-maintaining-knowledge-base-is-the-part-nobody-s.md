## Verbatim Text

The compounding math on a self-maintaining knowledge base is the part nobody's running.

Day 1: you feed 10 articles into the system. The LLM produces 15 wiki pages, maybe 8,000 words of structured, interlinked content. Takes about 20 minutes of compute time. You did zero writing.

Day 30: you've added 3 sources per week. The wiki now has 60+ pages. The LLM has been cross-referencing every new source against everything it already organized. Connections you'd never make manually are sitting there in the backlinks. A paper from week 1 contradicts a blog post from week 3, and the LLM flagged it.

Day 90: 100+ articles, 400,000 words. This is where Karpathy's wiki sits. You can ask it complex questions that require synthesizing information across dozens of sources, and it answers from pre-compiled knowledge instead of searching raw documents from scratch every time.

The traditional alternative: reading those 100 articles took roughly 50 hours. Taking notes on them took another 25. Cross-referencing and organizing those notes into a useful system would take another 50+ hours that you'll never spend because life intervenes. Total human labor for a comparable knowledge base: 125+ hours, and it degrades the moment you stop maintaining it.

The LLM Wiki: 100 articles ingested at maybe 5 minutes each of human curation time. 8 hours total. The other 117 hours of work happen automatically, and the system maintains itself indefinitely.

That 117-hour gap is the real product. And it grows with every source you add.
