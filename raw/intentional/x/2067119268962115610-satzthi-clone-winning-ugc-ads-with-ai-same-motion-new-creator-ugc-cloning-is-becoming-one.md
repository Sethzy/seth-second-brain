Clone Winning UGC Ads With AI — Same Motion, New Creator

UGC cloning is becoming one of the most useful workflows inside Pixalto.app.

Most AI video tools can generate a new ad from a prompt.

But for real UGC ads, prompt-only generation is not enough.

Why?

Because UGC is not just about the face or the product.

It is about:

• hand movement
• camera motion
• body posture
• product placement
• natural timing
• small pauses
• expression flow
• creator-style performance

So we built the Pixalto UGC Clone workflow differently.

Instead of asking the model to “make an ad”, Pixalto uses a reference UGC video as the motion blueprint.

The workflow takes:

1. A reference UGC video

2. A character or creator image

3. A product image

Then Pixalto analyzes the reference video structure and recreates the same style of performance with the new character and product.

The important part is motion consistency.

The system prepares the source video, extracts visual reference frames, creates a timestamp-based motion map, understands where the product appears, and then generates a video that follows the same camera flow, gestures, and scene rhythm.

For product videos, this matters a lot.

A product should not randomly change shape, disappear, float, resize, or drift during hand movements.

So the workflow gives special attention to product-visible moments, hand-object contact, close-ups, seated shots, surface placement, and motion blur areas.

Behind the scenes, Pixalto combines:

• reference video motion analysis
• product visual understanding
• character reference preservation
• prompt generation for video editing
• motion-controlled video generation
• optional voice change
• optional caption generation
• final video processing

The goal is simple:

Take a real UGC performance and recreate it for a new product or brand without reshooting everything.

This is useful for:

• TikTok ads
• Instagram Reels
• app promotions
• product demos
• creator-style ads
• eCommerce creatives
• localized ad variations
