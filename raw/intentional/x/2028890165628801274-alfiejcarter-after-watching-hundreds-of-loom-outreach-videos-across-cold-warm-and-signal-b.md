After watching hundreds of Loom outreach videos across cold, warm, and signal-based accounts, 

I built a single playbook we actually use.

If you want Loom to get paying clients - not just get views - this is the shortcut.

Inside you'll find:

✦ Target Accounts

(A 6-step system to identify the 50-100 accounts actually worth personalising for. Score each one on value potential and win likelihood. Then run a "personalization-ready" filter - because if you can't show anything on screen, your Loom becomes a talking head pitch. That loses.)

✦ Topic Discovery

(A 3-layer research method to find video topics prospects already want to watch. Layer 1: find the trigger - a recent change that makes a problem urgent. Layer 2: map the workflow closest to money and find where it breaks. Layer 3: attach proof so the insight feels safe to act on. If you can't answer all three, don't record yet.)

✦ Outreach Angles

(6 battle-tested script templates - Competitor Intel, Workflow Breakdown, Opportunity Analysis, Case Study Walkthrough, Market Insight, and Quick Win. Each one is structured around personal proof, a show moment, one insight, one outcome, and a soft next step. Nothing that sounds like a pitch.)

✦ Video Structure

(The 4-part framework that gets 60-90 second videos watched in full. Hook in the first 10 seconds - prove it's not generic. Show something real on screen by second 35. Deliver one insight, not five. Close with a CTA that feels easy to say yes to. If the video feels even slightly salesy, rerecord it slower.)

✦ Link Message

(3 message variants for sending the Loom link - one for cold, one for warm, one for post-connection. The difference between a message that gets clicked and one that gets ignored is context, permission, and relevance. Templates included for all three.)

Every section includes:

→ The exact "why" behind each decision, not just the tactic
→ Checklists to use before you record and before you send
→ Scripts with {variables} ready to fill in for your specific context

No "just be authentic" advice.

No vague frameworks with no execution path.

No 10-minute videos to watch before you get the actual thing.
