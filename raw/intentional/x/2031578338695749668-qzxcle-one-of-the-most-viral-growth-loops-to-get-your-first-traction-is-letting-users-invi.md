## Verbatim Text

One of the most viral growth loops to get your first traction is letting users invite users. It works because:

> FOMO bias urging action (people join what is visibly growing and fear missing potential upside) 
> Credibility bias reassuring users this is legitimate (when credible experts and KOLs signal "this is real")

Dropbox's two-sided referral program (invite a new user and both get extra storage) resulted in 34x growth spike over two years. PayPal incentivized referrals, achieving around 8% daily growth. Airbnb reduced "stranger danger" with reputation systems (reviews, profile signals, trust cues).

Drawing on this idea, I see a few ways to use it for bootstrapping a betting platform (for example):

> Two-sided referral reward. Inviter gets $1 in betting credits and the invitee $0.50 upon signing up and making a qualifying transaction (to counter bots, the reward can be a percentage of the invitee's traded volume instead). 

> Registration bonuses. Free bets, boosted first bet percentage return, no-loss first bet, deposit match. 

> KOL promos. Allow rev share on volume brought to the platform, plus a flat fee per new user who crosses $1 in trading volume. Throw in early access mechanics for them to distribute codes to their communities ahead of the public, creating an additional FOMO push. Here the bigger goal would be placement in major broadcasts and commentary cycles, so that market data becomes something journalists and analysts cite regularly. 

> Live leaderboard tracking P&L, biggest wins, and highest volume bets. It feeds directly into a conversion mechanism (like a $1 free bet voucher framed as a chance to win $10, displayed as a big green button). 

> New users receive status labels tied to activity milestones: "Early Bird," "Analyst," "Insider," "Grandmaster." Status creates identity, and identity drives retention. You tend to hold on to what makes you feel special. Broadly, it's a tiered VIP system that gives access to faster withdrawals, higher limits, dedicated account managers, exclusive markets, better cashback rates, etc. 

> Early adopters who join via invites receive lifetime zero-fee trading. Invites are distributed exclusively through KOLs and ref/invite links. 

> And some cool random benefits like weekly cashback on net losses, 2x on deposits made on Monday (or any other day, it wouldn't matter as long as it creates a sticky habit of returning to the platform on a regular basis), extra percentage on a winning bet streak.

So the idea is to make the viral loop a core product feature. Even if it's a temporary growth spike, that'd be enough to bootstrap the bootstrap.
