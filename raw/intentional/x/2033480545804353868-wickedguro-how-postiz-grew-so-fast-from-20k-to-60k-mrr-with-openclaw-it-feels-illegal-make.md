Funny thing about life, you don't know what's going to happen, but when something does, your first reaction can change your entire life.

When OpenClaw came out, I was not sure exactly what it was. Was it a hype/trend or something that's going to last, because let's face it, until you have real business use-cases, it's all a very nice game.

So I played the game that bumped [Postiz](https://postiz.io)'s MRR to 60k.

## A month ago, I was extremely lucky

I don't use social media listening tools; I have like 4 channels that I track: X / LinkedIn / YouTube. So every day (like a crazy Stripe dashboard refresher), I go to X, search for "[Postiz](https://postiz.io)," and click "latest".

Back then, I saw this guy @oliverhenry, he had, I think, 1000 followers? writing about [Larry](https://github.com/OllieWazza/LarrySkill), his old OpenClaw computer, and how he gets tons of views on TikTok for his app, Snuggly, and I think, like a few times, he mentioned [Postiz](https://postiz.io), but just very briefly.

In my head, I thought, "Wow, he is an amazing storyteller." And he said that he is going to write an article on X about how he does his TikTok stuff.

I was intrigued, but still in my mind, I thought. Well, he has like 1000 followers. I know the X algorithm. Probably, he will get like 10k views on his article, and if he mentions [Postiz](https://postiz.io), I might get 1-2 subscriptions. It would be cool.

And then he posted the article (and mentioned "[Postiz](https://postiz.io)" like 9 times), and it got 7 million views (wtf?). And thus began the age of the agentic [Postiz](https://postiz.io).

From the usual around 100 trials per week, I was at a whopping 800 trials in 3-4 days.

I have never talked to Oliver before (obviously, I did after this huge article), and we do many things together today. But who would have thought?

I won't lie to you, without Oliver, I don't think [Postiz](https://postiz.io) could explode like that. [Postiz](https://postiz.io) was already making $21k MRR and was also mentioned on Starter Story, but had slow growth.

I think the whole narrative of naming your OpenClaw, writing the article "together", like each paragraph somebody else writes, giving real proof of views and sales, is just a super good story.

It's not just "giving the sauce" it's also giving it with a story.

My trajectory was to finish the year with $30k-$40k MRR.
Now, there is not trajactory, no idea when the fast growth will stop.

## Growing way too fast

I know for some people it sounds like the dream, and it kind of is, you know, grabbing almost $60k to your pocket every month? But business-wise, it's stressful; I am still trying to figure it out.

- How much can my server scale?

- How to hire somebody for customer support?

- How to deal with the insane amount of messages I get?

Like they say: more money, more problems, and when the money is really fast, you get so many problems.

Like, literally, TikTok didn't post to people because they capped me with 100 active users per month. I sent them an email, and it took 3-4 days to fix it.

## Fast learning and adopting

One week later, Oliver posted another article, and this time, it got 1.3 million views (compared to 7 million in the previous one). But it brought a lot more conversions to [Postiz](https://postiz.io).

Why? In this article, Oliver posted a SKILL, not just the step-by-step algorithm of what to do, like in the previous article.

And why is this important?

Because people don't like to think. They want the "fast juice". And Oliver took the entire first article and turned it into an SKILL that people can install and start making money.

So he created a skill that generates an image slideshow, adds text to it, schedules it to TikTok, and then learn between posts and posts a better post for you. (and had a proof to back it up, that it works)

Part of the SKILL is to install [Postiz](https://postiz.io). And of course, there is also a 7-day trial, so you can just mess with it. I guess it really worked for people, and I experienced almost no churn.

Two main things were clear to me:

- Send any person I know to the [Larry skill](https://github.com/OllieWazza/LarrySkill), or [Larry's brain](https://www.larrybrain.com) (Oliver's new SaaS), and not directly to a [Postiz](https://postiz.io) skill.

- Try to scale the "article" thing because there is clearly some growth hack here, very similar to TikTok.

## Articles are extremely targeted

I am not exposed to the X algorithm. By the beginning of the year, we had already started to see many people write articles to compete in X's 1-million-dollar competition to determine which articles get the most views. Sadly, some crypto guy won it with a 48 million-view article. And, as far as I know, Crypto Twitter is mostly fake.

But...

It means they installed something specifically for articles, people spend more time reading articles, hence spend more time on the platform.

Since I read Oliver's articles, every time I opened my phone (specifically, my phone, not my web), I saw tons of OpenClaw articles on the main feed, all with unusually high view counts and lots of saves.

Even I (back then, 200 followers) wrote an article that got 500k views:

[Embedded Tweet: https://x.com/i/status/2025967492359913862]

And I started to feel the opportunity you have on TikTok.
TikTok, not so long ago, started to focus on content rather than on follower numbers. So if you have a really good video, even without followers, you can get a lot of views.

It's a bummer for people who worked hard for years to get a lot of followers, but it's good for me.

So I started contacting people on X whose last article got really high engagement and offered them a paid partnership, plus a lifetime [Postiz](https://postiz.io) account so they can actually play with it and show results.

It was a really good deal for me because it was long-form content from a small-to-mid-sized account at a super affordable price.

With a very low risk, because articles just work.

Around a maximum of $1k per article, it really depends on the risk for the article to trend.

For me, it's amazing because it's long form. People buy from long form. Short-form is a nice way to get followers/likes to your long-form.

Not that short form can't convert (it can, especially for b2c), but I prefer people to come with much more context, so that they know what they are going to do. that reduces churn - a lot.

And it worked, I managed to have a consistent 400 trials per month for [Postiz](https://postiz.io) + a lot of word of mouth

I think this is my "super power" as someone who has never had a big social media audience. I can try to use other people's power to grow my product.

I am really good at small freelance marketing hires.

I am really bad at hiring, desperately trying to hire a support engineer now, but it seems impossible in the land of AI Slop.

## Why Postiz and not the alternatives?

I think it's clear that [Postiz](https://postiz.io) controls the narrative (at least on X) of agentic social media.

Yes, some competitors also managed to crash it, but not as [Postiz](https://postiz.io).
And I don't think it's really only marketing (even though I'll admit I am super lucky).

- Open-source: [Postiz](https://postiz.io) has been fully open-source from day 1, for almost 2 years now. It allows you to self-host the solution if you don't want to pay, or pay $29 per month for the starter package. So more audience can use it, if they don't want to pay me. But it also makes me closer to DEVs. You see, OpenClaw is not for my father, the photographer. It's for technical business owners, and, being open-source, API-first, and CLI-first, it feels much closer to them.

- Making an early, fast decision: I was the first of my competitors to offer a SKILL and a CLI. I have had an MCP for more than a year now. After I used [agent-browser](https://github.com/vercel-labs/agent-browser), the CLI thing just clicked for me. It's faster, with less context and less context rot.

- Growth Hacking: Since the early days of [Postiz](https://postiz.io), I have been finding cool marketing hacks on Reddit, TikTok, LinkedIn, and other platforms. In general, I consider myself a growth hacker (someone who does small things that can bring you a lot of attention) rather than someone who consistently builds an audience.

- Skill as a lead-magnet: sending people to the Larry Skill with everything inside is much, much better than a step-by-step algorithm. kinda like an n8n template.

- Tried OpenClaw early on: I didn't just use OpenClaw for marketing on my articles; I actually use it in my day-to-day.

- Adapting fast: I already produced Claude Cowork articles and will soon produce them for Perplexity computer, too. hypes don't die. They change. Be the first, don't hold back!

## We are entering the personal chat assistant era, and it's going to grow a lot

I am not just saying this as a marketing thing. I actually think the CLI is a huge marketing opportunity in 2026.

This is why I am also building [agent-media](https://agent-media.ai) now.
It allows you to create a talking head with b-roll with a simple CLI command.

I think that doing everything through chat is the future.

For example:

- You load trending TikTok videos using [@virlomain](https://x.com/@virlomain) or topyappers

- You generate a UGC  based on the content

- [Postiz](https://postiz.io) schedule this to TikTok

- a daily cron checks the analytics and learn

- You create more UGCs for TikTok.

It's very similar to the [Larry skill](https://github.com/OllieWazza/LarrySkill), just instead of slideshows, it generates UGCs.

I think those small growth hacks won't last forever, because once something works, everybody does it until it doesn't. So be fast :)
