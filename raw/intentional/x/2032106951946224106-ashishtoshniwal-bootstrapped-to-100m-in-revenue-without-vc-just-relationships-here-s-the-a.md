Bootstrapped to $100M in revenue without VC. Just relationships. Here’s the AI playbook.

I have prepared for this moment for over 15 years.

At my previous startup, which I bootstrapped to ~$100M in revenue and 600 people. We started at the bottom of the financial crisis in March 2009.

We shipped our first iPhone app, which was the 55th app on the App Store.

Something magical happened on Aug 1, 2010, at 2.58 AM, we got this email -

"I love what you are doing! Thank You. Let me know if we can help.

Steve

sjobs@apple.com "

Life was never the same after. We became the team to help other enterprises ( 47 Fortune 500 companies ) envision their consumer experience as mobile-first.

We had one of the highest client retention rates in the industry, with individual account sizes of $72M across 5+ years. Our retention of top employees was very strong, considering Facebook and Google were in our backyard.

Hiring or retaining just 2–4 key people helped the company grow 7 times in the last five years to 600 people.

But at that scale, I couldn't even remember every top performer's name.

People have always asked me: "How do you manage your time and relationships?" The truth is, most leaders feel they don't have enough time and can't keep up with their relationships. But relationships are the real leverage. You have to remember the small details. You have to show up even when you don't need them. People always remember how you made them feel — not what you did.

I had a system for it. It was manual. It was tedious. My executive assistant, Danira, helped me run it, along with a team. It was about timing — not too early, not too late. The right detail, right before the meeting. Connecting people to the right opportunity. I was chatting with an entry-level engineer and a Fortune 500 CEO — all in the same day.

There were months when I held no internal meetings other than leadership — because we were behind on targets and I couldn't afford to be out of sight of clients. Danira told me: "Ashish, you spend 60% of your time in product reviews, 1-1s, and hiring — how will you hit your target? You need to be in front of the client."

> Today, you don't need a massive team and a complex process. I've distilled my playbook to build relationship capital and manage your time into @HeyNoahAI. You can check out the product in beta here - www.heynoah.io 
 
How does Hey Noah work

> Noah is the world's first executive AI agent, exclusively designed for very busy executives.

> I want to reiterate that it is ONLY for very busy people juggling different stakeholders, such as customers, teams, and networking events. It is for people who are in meetings all day long and constantly switching context like founders, executives, sales professionals and entrepreneurs.
 
Noah learns from your actions and habits, and you can set preferences just like how you talk to a human. There is no app or software to download. Noah lives in your SMS. Noah does not need inbox access. Our philosophy has been to build trust gradually rather than ask for all the data up front. 
 
It takes less than 10 seconds to onboard. Some of our customers call Noah as the CEO Claw !

Connect with Granola Notion, Google drive and follow up on your behalf and set up meetings. 

You show up and leave. Noah takes care of everything.
