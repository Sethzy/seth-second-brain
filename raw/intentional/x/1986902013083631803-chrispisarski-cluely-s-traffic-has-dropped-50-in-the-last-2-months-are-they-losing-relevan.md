## Verbatim Text

Cluely’s traffic has dropped 50% in the last 2 months. Are they losing relevance? 

After peaking in August with 2.8M monthly website visitors, Cluely’s seen a sudden 50% drop to 1.5M visitors in October.

In that same window, the startup pivoted from a viral “cheating assistant” to a more practical AI notetaker for meetings.

Roy, the founder, openly admits the playbook:

“The whole idea was let’s launch something that barely works, and if we can get enough initial users, they will find out the use cases for us.”

Cluely figured out how to get eyeballs on them but their declining traffic and recent pivot suggests the company’s not found PMF yet.

Pivoting into an AI notetaker is their latest bet. But with the market already filled with solid AI notetakers like Granola, incumbents like Gong offering their own notetaking features, it’ll be hard for Cluely to breakthrough.

Is another AI notetaker the way to go for Cluely - to stay relevant and actually become a business or will they be another startup that didn’t live up to their hype?
