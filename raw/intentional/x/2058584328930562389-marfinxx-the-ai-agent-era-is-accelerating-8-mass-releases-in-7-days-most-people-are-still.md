## 1. Cursor Composer 2.5 - The New Coding King

Cursor released Composer 2.5. It is their most capable agentic model yet, optimized for long-running multi-file tasks

[Embedded Tweet: https://x.com/i/status/2056415413077233983]

[ the details ]:

- Base: Built on Moonshot's Kimi K2.5

- Training: Scaled using reinforcement learning (RL) with text-based credit assignment over hundreds of thousands of tokens

- Math: 85% of its compute comes from additional RL and post-training. Kimi K2 and K2.5 only make up 7.5% of the compute each

- Pricing: Cursor doubled the free usage limit for the launch week

[ the metrics ]:

- CursorBench v3.1 (Hard tasks): Composer 2.5 hits 63.2%, beating GPT-5.5 medium (59.2%) and Composer 2 (52.2%), almost matching Claude Opus 4.7 xhigh (64.8%)

- Cost: Incredibly efficient. The average cost per task is near $0.50, compared to Opus 4.7 max ($11.00) or Opus 4.7 xhigh ($7.00). That's a 14x cost reduction for near-peak performance

- SWE-Bench Multilingual: Hits 79.8%, beating GPT-5.5 (77.8%) and Composer 2 (73.7%)

- Terminal-Bench 2.0: Composer 2.5 hits 69.3%, almost matching Opus 4.7 (69.4%)

This was the most unexpected release for me this week. To be honest, I didn’t expect @cursor_ai to be able to release a model that could compete with GPT-5.5 and Claude Opus 4.7. I hope they keep up the good work, and of course, we’re looking forward to API access

## 2. Google I/O 2026 - Google Goes All-In on Agents

Google delivered a massive presentation focused entirely on agent workflows, deep product integration, and monetization

[Embedded Tweet: https://x.com/i/status/2056815830634004944]

[ the details ]:

- Gemini 3.5 Flash: Ultra-fast model optimized for parallel agent tasks, now live in APIs and Android Studio

- Gemini Omni: Multimodality that supports consistent character generation across video scenes

- Google Flow: Gemini Omni Flash integration allowing users to "vibe code" complex video editing and effects

- Antigravity 2.0: A desktop agent platform with CLI, SDK, and real-time voice, running dozens of background tasks

- Gemini Spark: A 24/7 background agent running on dedicated VMs in Google Cloud, acting even when the laptop is closed

- Mobile AI Studio: Separate Android app for building and deploying agents from your phone

The biggest news of the past week. Google has really given us a lot of different releases. We’re really looking forward to the Gemini 3.5 Pro next month. All in all, I think this is a huge step for Gemini in terms of conquering the market

## 4. Qwen 3.7 Max - The Autonomous Office Assistant

Alibaba launched Qwen3.7-Max, an open-weights model built specifically for autonomous, long-horizon tasks

[Embedded Tweet: https://x.com/i/status/2057450220708147250]

[ the details ]:

- Long-Horizon Autonomy: Proven to run up to 35 hours fully autonomously. It executed over 1,000 tool calls without human intervention during kernel optimization tests

- Tool Integration: Deep support for MCP and multi-agent orchestration

- Compatibility: Scaffold-agnostic. Runs seamlessly inside Claude Code and OpenClaw

[ the metrics ]:

- IFBench (Instruction Following): Hits 79.1%, leading GLM-5.1 (76.0%) and Claude Opus-4.6 Max (62.5%)

- SWE-bench Pro: Hits 60.6%, beating DS-V4-Pro Max (59.0%) and Claude Opus-4.6 Max (57.3%)

- HLE (Humanity's Last Exam): Hits 41.4%, leading DS-V4-Pro Max (37.7%) and Claude Opus-4.6 Max (40.0%)

- Apex (Math Reasoning): Hits 44.5%, completely crushing the competition (Qwen3.6-Plus is 8.8%, Kimi K2.6 is 34.5%)

- MCP-Atlas: Qwen 3.7 Max hits 76.4%, leading GLM-5.1 (66.6%) and Claude Opus-4.6 Max (75.8%)

A powerful release from @Alibaba_Qwen. They continue to maintain a high standard, even when compared to proprietary models. In a word - impressive

## 7. OpenAI Erdős Proof - AI Solves Open Math Problems

OpenAI announced that their reasoning model autonomously solved Paul Erdős’s planar unit distance problem from 1946

[Embedded Tweet: https://x.com/i/status/2057176201782075690]

[ the details ]:

- Rather than a specialized system, this was achieved by a general-purpose reasoning model

- The model's Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning path spanned 125 pages

- It found a new family of geometric structures that beat 80 years of human-designed grid structures

Wow, that's really cool. People are already predicting that AI will soon be winning Nobel Prizes. It's both scary and fascinating at the same time
