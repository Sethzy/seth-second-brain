## Verbatim Text

After Andromeda, Advantage+, AI UGC, and everything coming with GEM, the winning framework on Meta comes down to this.

1- Diversity over volume.
50 versions of the same ad is one ad. 
Five genuinely different angles is five ads. 
Andromeda rewards distinct approaches, not minor color swaps.
If swapping two creatives doesn't change the message, you have one creative. 
The algorithm needs real variation, not wallpaper.

2- Hooks in 0.8 seconds.
That's how fast the first frame has to land.
Visual contrast. One focal point. Instant emotion.
Polished produced video is losing to raw, native, phone-shot content for exactly this reason. 
The brain isn't deciding whether your ad is well-crafted. It's deciding whether to keep scrolling. That decision happens before your headline even loads.

3- AI for volume. Humans for trust.
AI UGC wins the top of the funnel. Hooks, variations, scroll-stoppers. Cheap, fast, infinite. Test 20 angles in a day.
Real UGC wins the bottom. Transformations. Product demos. Niche-recognized creators. Emotional testimonials. Trust converts. AI doesn't.
Don't ask AI to do what only a real person can. 
Don't pay a creator for what AI does free.

4-  Five angles per persona, not five versions of one angle.
Same product, five reasons to buy:
Frustration. "Tired of X. Nothing's worked." 
Aspiration. "This is what the next version of you looks like." 
Skeptic. "I didn't believe it until I tried it." 
Insider. "What the industry doesn't tell you." 
Identity. "This is for people who think differently."
Five angles, three formats, three hook variations. That's 45 genuinely different creatives from one product. That's the volume Andromeda actually wants.

5- Refresh every 2-3 weeks.
Advantage+ accelerates fatigue. The "build one winner, scale it for six months" model is dead.
By the time a creative is winning, your next batch should already be in production. 
Not concepting. Production.

The decision layer.
AI made every layer above this cheap. Generation. Variation. Production. All cheap.
The only thing left that isn't cheap is the decision.
Which angle is worth testing. Which hook actually lands. Which creative is technically optimized and creatively dead. Which one is ugly on purpose and converting because of it.
That's the work now. AI handles the layers. You handle the decisions.
Creative is 70% of results in 2026. The other 30% is everything Meta automates.
That ratio used to be reversed.
