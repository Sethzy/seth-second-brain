## Verbatim Text

Founder-led content ONLY works if you build an operating layer around the founder.

The founder cannot maintain the cadence alone.

We run 4 pillars at workflows. 

These are the layers wired for our own founders and installed for clients running founder-led inbound:

Pillar 1: Voice calibration
→ Capture how the founder talks and turn it into a structured guide every other person on the team can write to.
→ The voice guide lives in a GitHub repo as a Markdown file.
→ It names the specific phrasings the founder uses, formats they favor on LinkedIn, topics they have a point of view on, and topics they refuse to write about.
→ The LinkedIn post writer skill in our Company OS Starter Kit reads this voice guide every time it drafts.

Pillar 2: Content cadence
→ The cadence floor is 2 LinkedIn posts a week, plus quarterly long-form content like newsletters or guest podcasts.
→ A 90-day content calendar maps topics to the funnel ahead of time.
→ Every post has a hook variation ready before the founder ever opens LinkedIn.

Pillar 3: Distribution and engagement
→ Posts do not distribute themselves. The team has to engage in the first 60 minutes.
→ Comments from team members in the first hour signal to the algorithm that the post deserves reach.
→ We track engagement on every post through Jungler and Trigify.
→ The founder also engages 20 minutes a day with their network's posts before shipping their own.

Pillar 4: Team brand expansion
→ One founder posting is the start. A team of personal brands is the multiplier.
→ Extend the voice guide and content calendar to senior team members.
→ Each person owns a sub-area: the founder, the GTM lead, the product lead, the engineering lead, the design lead.
→ The brand fans out across categories without diluting because every account stays in the same operating system.

Personal brand authority takes 6 to 12 months to add up.

But better late than never...
