## Verbatim Text

Top 1% agencies aren't writing more cold emails. But they're booking more demos than you.

I spent 3 months analyzing 500k cold emails from agencies doing $1M+ ARR. Here's what separates the winners from everyone else:

The structure is completely different from what most cold email gurus online teach you.

Standard cold email structure: 

→ Pain point (generic) 
→ Solution (your service) 
→ Social proof (we helped X get Y) 
→ CTA (book a call)

This gets 0.8% reply rates. Maybe 0.3% positive.

Top 1% structure: 

→ Specific observation about their business (proves you did research) 
→ Single tactical insight (give value upfront) 
→ What happens if they ignore it (creates urgency without being pushy) 
→ Exit (soft CTA or no CTA at all)

This gets 3-5% reply rates. 1-2% book calls.

You're leading with intelligence, not desperation. 

You're diagnosing their problem better than they can. 

And you're not asking for anything which makes them want to reply.

Example:

"Noticed your site runs Webflow with a generic contact form and Calendly for demos. 

Most teams with this setup send all outbound traffic to the same landing experience. 

So cold prospects hit a form that asks for info you already know. That friction alone drops conversion 20-30%. 

Instead, send outbound traffic to a stripped-down scheduling page with prefilled fields. Same offer. Half the steps. 

Teams usually see 20% higher show rates without touching copy."

Fix how you open, and the close takes care of itself.
