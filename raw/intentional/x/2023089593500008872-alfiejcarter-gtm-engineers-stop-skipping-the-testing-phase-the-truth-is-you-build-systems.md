## Verbatim Text

GTM Engineers:

STOP skipping the testing phase.

The truth is, you build systems step by step.

- Subreddit selection can be validated (50K+ members, ICP check, culture analysis).
- Comment structures can be tested (sandwich format at position 3 of 5 tips).
- Email sequences can be optimized (Offer → Delivery → Case Study).

What's harder to fix...

No qualification layer in your lead magnet.

Zero engagement tracking on downloads.

Posting product links and getting banned.

Sending 200 downloads with 5 replies and 0 meetings.

No follow-up strategy tied to behaviour.

Some of the best pipeline I've seen didn't come from perfect execution.

It came from systems that qualified while delivering value.

They tracked which sections prospects engaged with.

They used topic selection to filter collectors from buyers.

They gave a 10-minute quick win first, then showed the full system, then revealed what scaling actually requires (where your service becomes the obvious solution).

If you're building serious GTM infrastructure, your framework should be strong enough to handle imperfect execution at first.

Because pipeline systems aren't just found.

They're built through testing.

So before you reject a channel for not being "ready-made," ask yourself:

Is the tactic broken?

Or are you missing the qualification and tracking layer?

Test the framework first.

It might be the best pipeline source you ever build.
