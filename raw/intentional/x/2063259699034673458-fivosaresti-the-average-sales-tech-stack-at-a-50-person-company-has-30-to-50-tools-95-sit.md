The average sales tech stack at a 50-person company has 30 to 50 tools.

95% sit unused.

Here are 17 categories of tools that are ACTUALLY useful in a 2026 GTM stack:

1) Signals → Clay, RB2B, Jungler, Fibbler
2) Data enrichment → Freckle, Clay, Apollo, Findymail, BetterContact
3) Automated sequencers → HeyReach for LinkedIn, Instantly for email
4) Sales rep sequencers → Nooks for parallel dialing, Apollo, HubSpot
5) Scraping and search → Serper, Apify, Exa, Browserbase, Firecrawl
6) Prospecting databases → Apollo, SalesNav, Ocean(.)io, AI Ark
7) Communication → Gmail, Slack, Loom
8) Automation and agents → n8n, Claude Code, Trigger(.)dev, Cargo
9) LLMs → Claude for most tasks, Gemini for images, ChatGPT for Claygent runs
10) Productivity → Wispr Flow for dictation, 1Password, Raycast, CleanShot X
11) Sales → HubSpot CRM, Qwilr, Superhuman, Kondo for LinkedIn DMs, Sybill
12) Marketing → Webflow, beehiiv, Customer(.)io, Tally, Ahrefs
13) Content → Figma, Miro, MagicPost, Ordinal, Riverside
14) Finance → Mercury, Stripe, Quickbooks, Brex
15) Operations → Notion, Airtable, GitHub, Cursor, Cloudflare
16) Documents → Google Drive, PandaDoc
17) Backend → Vercel, Supabase, Pinecone, DigitalOcean

Notice what is missing...

Standalone copywriting tools used to sit in content
→ They moved into the LinkedIn post writer skill and the outbound copywriter skill.

Manual research tools shrank
→ Claude Code can now run most one-off research through Firecrawl and Serper.

A 30-tool stack used well outperforms a 60-tool stack used at 30 percent.
