10 Realistic Ways to Make Money During the 2026 FIFA World Cup Using AI

The 2026 FIFA World Cup runs from June 11 to July 19, 2026, across 16 host cities: 11 in the USA, 3 in Mexico, 2 in Canada.

It is the first 48-team World Cup, with 104 matches over 39 days.

FIFA projects roughly 6 billion engagements this year, that's n increase of about 20% over Qatar 2022's officially verified 5 billion, building on the 1.5 billion viewers who watched the Argentina–France 2022 final.

39 days of concentrated global attention and this guide maps 10 honest ways to earn from that attention using AI tools, things you can start this week with a phone or laptop, from anywhere, with no upfront capital.

AI here is a time-saver and an output-multiplier. It writes first drafts, makes images, clones voices, edits video, and translates, fast and cheap.

but we also have to remember  that it isn't magic, and it doesn't replace having something useful to sell. Every idea below has a real track record from the 2018 and 2022 tournaments and i intentionally filtered out  ideas that sound good but have failed in practice.

## The 10 Ways + why each of them made the cut

1. Unofficial fan merch via print-on-demand : Merch demand skyrockets every World Cup and POD needs zero inventory or capital.

2. Digital downloads and printables : these materials sell worldwide with no shipping and near-100% margins.

3. A World Cup niche content site : Search traffic for fixtures, "how to watch" and host-city guides also pops off, and AI lets one person run this.

4. Faceless short-form video (previews and predictions) : Short clips are the fastest way to reach fans, and AI removes the need to film or appear on camera.

5. A faceless YouTube explainer channel : Long-form explainers earn far more per view than shorts and keep earning after the World Cup ends.

6. "World Cup marketing kit" services for local businesses : Bars, cafés and shops everywhere want promo material now, and they pay humans, not platforms.

7. Content localization and subtitling : A tournament in three countries across many languages creates instant demand to translate and caption content.

8. A prediction and analysis blog with betting affiliates : Bettors search constantly during the tournament, and you can earn from legal content without ever taking a bet.

9. Selling templates and design assets to other creators : In a gold rush, simply sell shovels, templates that other sellers and creators buy to save time.

10. Hyperlocal watch-party promotion and social media : Every neighborhood has viewing centers, and they need publicity to drive people in.

What I cut, and why ┐

- Physical dropshipping of jerseys and gadgets (needs capital, slow shipping, customs, and it was badly oversaturated in 2022)

- Operating any betting, fantasy or sweepstakes service (needs gambling licenses, illegal in most places without one)

- Ticket reselling (breaks FIFA's terms and many scalping laws)

- Selling counterfeit or logo merchandise (trademark infringement, which FIFA actively issues takedowns for)

- "AI crypto/trading" World Cup schemes (mostly scams); and building a big personal influencer brand from zero (takes many months, so you would miss the tournament entirely).

Each of these fails at least one hard rule : no capital, no licenses, accessible anywhere, and must pay off inside the World Cup window.

## 1. Unofficial Fan Merch via print-on-demand

Why the World Cup creates demand

Fans buy things to wear their colors. Across past tournaments, fan apparel, flags, scarves and novelty accessories rank as the top-selling product categories, and demand follows a sharp, predictable curve: search volume for football fan merchandise peaks two to three weeks before the opening match, holds through the knockout rounds, and collapses after the final. Sellers report orders spiking 48 to 72 hours before big matches. With 48 teams instead of 32, there are 16 more national fanbases buying gear in 2026: more niches, more designs, more buyers.

How AI helps

You do not need to draw. Use ChatGPT or Claude (free tiers work) to brainstorm slogans for specific fanbases. "Twin mom Halloween shirt" beats "mom shirt," and the same logic applies to "Morocco fan dad" versus "soccer fan." Use Canva's free AI tools, Google's Gemini image generator (usable free tier), or Midjourney ($10/month basic) to make clean graphics: flag color blocks, country-name typography, a generic trophy silhouette, a number-10 jersey motif. AI mockup generators show the design on a shirt so you never buy a sample blind.

step-by-step

1. Pick a print-on-demand platform that holds no inventory and pays you the margin: Redbubble or Teespring/Spring (they handle everything, lowest effort) or Printful/Gelato connected to an Etsy or Shopify store (more control, more setup).

2. Choose three to five fan angles: a host-country mashup, two or three popular national teams, one funny "neutral fan" design.

3. Generate designs with AI; keep them simple. Single-element graphics print cleanly and look like real fan gear, not rushed knockoffs.

4. List now with keyword-rich titles ("Argentina soccer fan shirt 2026," not "cool tee").

5. Promote free via short videos of the designs on TikTok and Instagram.

You can be live within a week.

Earning Potential

- Conservative : $50–$300 over the tournament from a handful of designs on Redbubble with no promotion.

- Moderate : $300–$2,000 if a couple of designs catch a fanbase and you push them on social.

- Best-case scenario : Several thousand dollars. For scale, CNBC verified (Sept. 2024, documents reviewed) that print-on-demand seller Iris Odio-Sutton's event-themed Etsy store brought in $220,300 in a year working about 10 hours a week, with roughly 30% profit. But that is a mature, year-round shop, not a one-month seasonal play.

Challenges and Risks

This is the most crowded idea on the list.

Everyone thinks of Merch first.

so if you are dealing with merch, you will compete with thousands of sellers and the legal line is very clear.

> you cannot use FIFA's logos, the official emblem, the trophy's exact design, team crests, or player names and likenesses.

so you have to stick to country colors, generic football imagery and your own slogans.

p.s : Margins on POD are thin (often a few dollars per item). And it's very time sensitive.

## 2. Digital Downloads and Printables

Why the World Cup creates demand

Some of the most-searched World Cup content is practical, people want a wall chart to track all 104 matches, a printable bracket for the office, something they can use to draw teams with friends and watch-party decorations. These are perfect digital products. Buyers want them instantly, and there's zero shipping, and the same one-time file (designed by AI) sells unlimited times.

The whole "three-country, 12-group, Round-of-32" format is new and confusing, which makes a clean, well-designed wall chart genuinely useful.

How AI helps

1 - Use ChatGPT/Gemini to generate the structure and copy (group line-ups, match dates, sweepstake rules).

2 - Use Canva (Free/Pro) to lay out the wall chart, bracket, bingo cards, coloring pages for kids, and party signs.

Step-by-Step

1. Open an Etsy shop (US$0.20 per listing) or a free Gumroad store.

2. Build a small bundle: a printable wall chart, a sweepstake kit, watch-party signs, and kids' coloring pages.

3. Deliver as PDF/JPG with a one-line instruction ("US Letter and A4 included").

4. Write keyword-rich titles ("World Cup 2026 wall chart printable PDF, all 104 matches").

5. Keep prices low ($3–$8) for impulse buys and bundle for higher order value.

Earning Potential

- Conservative : $30–$200 over the tournament.

- Moderate: $200–$1,500 if your wall chart ranks for a good search term.

- Best-case scenario : A few thousand dollars from a top-ranking listing.

@TheHustle reported that 20-year-old Erika Cea in the Philippines sold an average of 300 Taylor Swift-themed digital downloads a month on Etsy, proof that a solo seller in a lower-income country can profit from a single global event with files alone.

Challenges and Risks

Same trademark rules

> no FIFA logos or official branding.

Call it "2026 soccer tournament" if needed.

p.s : Etsy printables are competitive, so your design quality and keywords decide whether you actually making money, not forgetting that demand evaporates the day after the final, so  you MUST front-load your listings.

## 3. A World Cup niche content site

Why the World Cup creates demand

Search interest is staggering. Google's search traffic hit its highest level ever recorded during the 2022 final, and CEO @sundarpichai called it the highest in the company's 25-year history.

The biggest growing searches during matches are practical

- "how to watch,"

- what channel,

- what time,

- fixtures.

For the 2018 tournament, FIFA reported 3 billion TV viewers worldwide, with official data recording a combined 3.572 billion unique viewers, basically a vast pool of people hunting for information online.

People want host-city guides, kick-off times in their timezone, and "where to watch in [city]."

One person with AI can serve that demand.

How AI helps

Use ChatGPT or Claude to draft articles fast ┐

- fixture guides,

- "how to watch in [country],"

- host-city travel and watch-party guides,

- team and player profiles.

Use AI for outlines and first drafts, then add real facts and edit (because AI can get dates and details wrong) and use free SEO tools and Google's own search suggestions to know what people type.

Step-by-Step

1. Start a free or cheap site (a free Blogger/WordPress.com site, or a low-cost domain).

2. Target practical, low-competition searches: "World Cup 2026 schedule [your country] time," "where to watch World Cup in [city]."

3. Publish steadily before and during the tournament.

4. Monetize with display ads (Google AdSense to start; @ezoic / @mediavine at higher traffic) and affiliate links (streaming trials, VPNs, hotels, fan gear).

Earning Potential

- Conservative : $20–$150 in ad revenue if you start late.

- Moderate : $150–$1,000 across the tournament with steady publishing and a few ranking pages.

- Best-case scenario : A few thousand dollars if you build authority early and capture high-traffic searches.

Challenges and Risks

p.s : SEO has become unstable, AI-generated search (GEO) answers are eating the clicks right now, and brand-new sites rank slowly, so you may not rank in time if you start in June. Google penalizes low-effort, mass-produced AI content, so weakly written pages will fail. The safer play here is to write fewer, genuinely useful pages (get an SEO guy or work hand in hand with a cracked SEO/GEO tool).

## 4. Faceless short-form video (previews and predictions)

Why the World Cup creates demand

Short video is how fans now consume football. Fans want ┐

- quick previews,

- predictions,

- "who will win Group X,"

- player explainers and reactions,

every single matchday for 39 days.

@GoogleTrends data from past tournaments shows search and attention spiking minute-by-minute around goals and key moments, which is exactly what short clips feed on.

How AI helps

You never have to show your face. Use ChatGPT to script a 60-second preview; ElevenLabs (free tier, or $5/month Starter for commercial rights) for a natural voiceover and CapCut (very generous free tier) to assemble it with captions and your own graphics, charts and effects. and you can also produce several clips a day.

Step-by-Step

1. Pick one platform to start : TikTok, Instagram Reels, or YouTube Shorts.

2. Choose a narrow angle : African teams, an underdog nation, daily predictions, or tactics explained simply.

3. Script, then AI voice, then CapCut edit with captions, then post daily during the tournament.

4. Monetize later via TikTok's Creator Rewards Program, affiliate links in bio (fan gear, your own printables from idea #2), and eventually brand deals.

Earning Potential

- Conservative : Near $0 directly at first. Short-form ad money is small and you need scale.

- Moderate : TikTok's Creator Rewards Program pays roughly $0.40–$1.00 per 1,000 qualified views (videos must be over one minute, and you need 10,000 followers plus 100,000 views in 30 days to qualify). A genuinely viral run could mean a few hundred dollars.

- Best-case scenario : $500–$2,000+ if one account takes off and you stack affiliate sales on top, since affiliate income usually beats ad income at small scale.

Challenges and Risks

This is extremely saturated and most accounts earn little. The biggest trap is copyright: do NOT upload match footage or broadcast clips. FIFA and broadcasters use Content ID, and rights holders sue. The English Premier League won a case against a UK YouTuber who used match footage. Use your own graphics, stills and commentary. Instagram's direct payouts are tiny and unreliable (some creators report as little as $0.01–$0.14 per 1,000 views), so treat Reels as reach, not income.

## 5. A Faceless YouTube explainer channel

Why the World Cup creates demand

Long-form video earns far more per view than shorts and keeps earning for months. Fans want

- 8–15 minute team histories,

- player profiles,

- "everything you need to know about Group F,"

- tactical breakdowns and tournament explainers.

Demand for World Cup explainer content runs for the whole build-up and tournament, and the videos keep getting views even after the final.

How AI helps

Faceless production is now cheap : ChatGPT for research and script, ElevenLabs for narration, and stock footage or AI visuals plus CapCut/Descript for editing. The faceless model works precisely because content quality matters more than personality in explainer niches.

Step-by-Step

1. Create a channel with a clear theme ("World Cup explained").

2. Publish two to four explainers a week : group previews, team profiles, "how the new 48-team format works." and all that.

3. Add affiliate links and your own digital products in the description from Day 1.

4. Apply to the YouTube Partner Program once eligible (1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours).

Earning Potential

- Conservative : $0 during this tournament. Most channels take 6–12 months to qualify for ad revenue. Awesome Creator Academy reports that less than 6% of channels are monetized in the YouTube Partner Program, and that 90% of channels never even reach 10,000 subscribers.

- Moderate : A monetized sports channel earns a low RPM (sports is a low-paying niche, roughly $1–$5 per 1,000 views versus $14–$38 for finance/tech), so $200–$1,000/month at modest scale.

- Best-case scenario : A channel that breaks out can earn several thousand a month, but that is rare and slow.

Challenges and Risks

p.s : this is the slowest idea here and likely will NOT pay during the 2026 window unless you already have a channel because Sports has low ad rates and YouTube demonetizes low-effort, mass-produced AI content. Treat it as a longer-term asset that the World Cup kick-starts, and lean on affiliate income early rather than waiting for AdSense.

## 6. "World Cup marketing kit" services for local businesses

Why the World Cup creates demand

This is the fastest way to actual cash, because clients pay you directly and you are not waiting on platform payouts.

Every bar, café, restaurant, barbershop and small shop wants to ride the tournament ┐

- watch-party flyers,

- social posts,

- a themed menu,

- a short promo video.

Most owners have no time or skill to make these but The World Cup gives them a reason to spend now.

How AI helps

You become a one-person agency. ChatGPT writes captions and promos, Canva designs flyers and menus, CapCut and Runway (free/$15 tier) make short promo videos, ElevenLabs voices them. What used to take a designer hours takes you minutes, so you can serve many clients.

Step-by-Step

1. Make three sample "World Cup watch-party" kits (flyer + 5 social posts + a 15-second video) to show.

2. Offer them on Fiverr and Upwork, and pitch local venues directly via DM or in person.

3. Sell a fixed package, "World Cup promo kit, $X."

4. Upsell weekly posting through the tournament for recurring income.

Earning Potential

- Conservative : $50–$300 from a few small local jobs.

- Moderate : On Fiverr, basic social video packages start around $60–$70 and premium ones run $200–$400; social content writing runs a median of about $25–$35/hour on Upwork. A handful of packages = $500–$2,000.

- Best-case scenario : $2,000–$5,000+ if you land several venues on weekly retainers for the 39 days.

Challenges and Risks

- You need basic sales skills and will have to apply them in person.

- Cold DMs/email outreach is uncomfortable and you'll get a  lot of big fat NO's.

- Quality matters. AI output needs your eye so it does not look generic.

- Clients can be slow to pay so take a deposit.

- aaannnndd, avoid putting FIFA logos on client material, do your best to keep it as generic as possible (this is where your creativity comes in)

## 7. Content localization and subtitling

Why the World Cup creates demand

This World Cup spans three countries and a huge multilingual audience.

North America alone has a massive Spanish-speaking fanbase, and creators worldwide want to reach English- and Spanish-speaking fans during the tournament.

@CSA_Research 's 2020 survey of 8,709 consumers across 29 countries found that 65% prefer content in their own language even when it is poor quality, and 76% prefer to buy products with information in their native language.

Captions and translated versions massively expand reach, and creators and small businesses will pay to get them fast.

How AI helps

AI has collapsed this cost. @DeepLcom (free tier) handles translation, CapCut auto-captions, ElevenLabs and tools like @Perso_AI can dub video into other languages while keeping the speaker's tone. all at a fraction of studio cost.

Step-by-Step

1. Pick a language pair you know well (e.g., English-Spanish, English-Arabic, English-French).

2. Offer services on Fiverr/Upwork : subtitle a video, translate a blog, dub a short clip.

3. Approach football creators and small brands who want to reach a second-language audience for the tournament.

4. Price per minute of video or per 1,000 words. (we don't want to burn too much money now, do we?)

Earning Potential

- Conservative : $50–$300 from a few subtitling gigs.

- Moderate : Freelance writing/translation runs roughly $15–$45/hour; steady gigs = $500–$2,000 over the tournament.

- Best-case scenario : $2,000–$4,000+ if you build a few repeat clients pushing daily content.

Challenges and Risks

AI translation makes mistakes, especially with slang and football idioms so your value is in fixing those, so do not just hand over raw machine output. (AI is supposed to improve efficiency not do your work for you).

Never dub or subtitle copyrighted match footage for clients, stick to original creator and brand content.

## 8. A Prediction and Analysis blog with betting affiliates

Why the World Cup creates demand

Betting interest around the World Cup is enormous and global, and bettors search these keywords constantly

- fixtures,

- form,

- predictions,

- "best bets today."

You can earn from this entirely legally by creating content (previews, analysis, prediction articles) and earning affiliate commissions when readers sign up to licensed sportsbooks, without ever operating a betting service yourself.

How AI helps

ChatGPT drafts match previews, team-form summaries and prediction articles at speed , you add real analysis and current data. AI also helps with SEO structure and social media posts to drive readers to your content.

Step-by-Step

1. Build a simple blog or a Telegram/Instagram channel focused on World Cup previews and predictions.

2. Join legitimate sports-betting affiliate programs in regions where it is legal (the US, where most states now allow it, plus the UK, Brazil, and other regulated markets).

3. Publish daily previews and prediction pieces (and place your affiliate links honestly.)

4. Always include responsible-gambling notes and follow each program's rules.

Earning Potential

- Conservative : $0–$200 if traffic is small.

- Moderate : Affiliate deals pay roughly $50–$200 per depositing referral (CPA) or 30–60% revenue share; a handful of conversions = a few hundred dollars.

- Best-case scenario : $1,000–$5,000+ if even a small audience converts, because each referral is worth a lot.

Challenges and Risks

This is heavily regulated and crowded. You must only promote licensed operators in markets where it is legal, follow advertising rules (age warnings, no misleading claims), and never target minors.

also,many platforms restrict gambling promotion, so a self-hosted blog is safer than relying on social reach. Don't present predictions as guaranteed because they are not.

When there's a gold rush, sell shovels.

Thousands of people are doing ideas 1–6, and they all need assets fast.

Canva templates for World Cup social posts, CapCut video templates, thumbnail packs and flyer templates for bars.

simple, you sell to the sellers.

Earning Potential

Challenges and Risks

- This idea is a competitive one, and templates can be copied.

## 10. Local watch-party promotion and social media

Why the World Cup creates demand

You do not need a national audience. You need your neighborhood. Every area has venues showing matches and fans looking for "where to watch." A 48-team tournament with fans from 48 nations means specific communities (a Moroccan café, a Korean restaurant, a Mexican sports bar) want to fill their place on matchdays. This is available in all countries, 1st, 2nd or 3rd world.

How AI helps

ChatGPT writes the posts and event listings, Canva makes the matchday graphics, CapCut does the short clips. You can run several venues' social media at once because AI handles the grunt work.

Step-by-Step

1. List local watch parties in free spots (Google Maps posts or local Facebook/Whatsapp groups) and build a simple "where to watch in [your city]" page.

2. Approach venues : offer to run their matchday posts and design their flyers for a flat fee.

3. Optionally monetize your own local guide with venue promotion fees or affiliate links.

4. Deliver daily matchday posts through the tournament.

Earning Potential

- Conservative : $50–$300 from one or two venues.

- Moderate : $300–$1,500 managing a few venues' social posts for the 39 days.

- Best-case scenario : $1,500–$4,000+ with several venues on matchday retainers.

Challenges and Risks

It takes a lot of IRL legwork and local trust/street cred. You have to show up or DM persistently. Results depend on your area's football interest. Venues may want proof it works, so start with one cheap or free trial to build a case study.

as always, keep the material generic and avoid implying official FIFA affiliation.

## The Honest Bottom Line

Two patterns repeat across 2018 and 2022 and will repeat in 2026 : the attention is massive but very short-lived, and the people who make money are the ones who prepare before kickoff and serve a specific, practical need.

The biggest single creator-era winner was Brazil's @CazeTVOficial , launched in November 2022 by streamer @Casimiro  with sports-marketing firm @LiveModeTV_PT , which gained 5.5 million subscribers streaming 22 matches of the 2022 World Cup and has since secured rights to all 104 matches of the 2026 tournament. But that was a funded operation with rights deals, not a solo play, and it is an exception/ outlier not a blueprint.

- For a solo beginner, the fastest cash is in services (ideas 6, 7, 10) because clients pay you directly and immediately.

- The best leverage is in digital products (ideas 2 and 9) because they sell even while you sleep.

- The most crowded and slowest are merch, short-form video and faceless YouTube, these 3 are still doable, but go in with caution.

across all of them, AI lowers the cost and speeds up the working process but like i said in the opening paragraph of this article, it doesn't create the demand or guarantee sales.
