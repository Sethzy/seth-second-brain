## Verbatim Text

I think this is a bit tricky. I agree the most central example is one where you are fully aware you are going to blow things up, but it's also pretty common for the person threatening to not be aware (or to just be kind of aware).

Building a "country of geniuses in a datacenter" is a very intense thing to do unilaterally by anyone's lights. Even if you just take the premise of being able to do so safely, and under your control, it's still an enormous threat (and in the absence of your ability to control it becomes a threat to blow up everything).

The central dynamic it is setting up is also saying that if China reacts to this with "well, I guess we have no choice but to race really hard ourselves", then if they succeed, then you do agree the risks will be really bad because then you do have a neck-to-neck race, and you will try to blame your adversaries on it. The basic dynamic, even just on the premises of the article itself is saying "racing is bad, indeed so bad that we have to win the race with as much margin as possible, because our enemies leave us no choice but to race, and also we will crush them when we win, and if it goes badly, it will be their default because they left us no option but to race".
