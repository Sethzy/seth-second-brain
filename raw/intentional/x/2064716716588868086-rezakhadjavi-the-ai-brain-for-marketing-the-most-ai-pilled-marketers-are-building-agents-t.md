## Verbatim Text

The AI Brain for Marketing

The most AI-pilled marketers are building agents that run 24/7 on a Mac Mini. They connect a dozen APIs, maintain a massive filesystem of markdown files and they use Claude Code or Hermes to get a ridiculous amount of work done.

They are building an AI Brain that is way more powerful than cramming files into a Claude Project.

It looks something like this:

```
# example AI brain for marketing

brain/
├── brand/
│   ├── positioning.md
│   ├── voice.md              # how the brand talks
│   └── claims.md
├── customers/
│   ├── personas.md
│   └── voice-of-customer.md  # real customer language
├── creative/
│   ├── winning-hooks.md      # what's worked, why
│   ├── swipe-file.md
│   └── strategy-engine.md    # the core framework
├── performance/
│   └── benchmarks.md         # what good looks like
└── routines/
    ├── nightly-report.md     # last night's numbers
    └── competitor-watch.md   # competitor ads, weekly

# ...and 200+ more files
```

LLMs are trained on mountains of code, so navigating a repository of markdown files is like second nature to them. Agents are also excellent at using the command line so when you hand them a full computer, they go ham. That's why the Mac Mini trend took off. It's the best way for an individual marketer to push AI to its limits.

## Where the Mac Mini breaks

The magic of the Mac Mini falls apart when you go from individual use to team (or client) collaboration. The technical setup is too daunting for most people and there is no clean way for everyone to work inside the same brain without tripping over each other.

So you end up with a brain that's locked away on one person's machine.

## The Sandbox Gold Rush

Giving AI a virtual computer is one of the biggest problems in the industry right now.

Anthropic launched Managed Agents earlier this year. Notion built Custom Agents. And a whole new category is booming around Sandboxes to give AI access to a computer to do real work on a team.

[Embedded Tweet: https://x.com/i/status/2058988943425577065]

## No one was building the Mac Mini in the cloud

We were dead set on giving our agent a computer, so we went sandbox shopping. We looked at the whole map of vendors. But we didn't find what we were looking for. The vendors were mostly renting out computers by the task. Spin one up, run a job, then wind it down for another agent to use. They weren't offering what makes the Mac Mini special: giving the agent a real, full computer to live on.

They had pieces of it... persistence here, sharing there, but we wanted a full machine, a dedicated one for each of our customers.

> So we said screw it and built it ourselves. It's called Runneth.

Use the Runneth CLI if you're intense, or just talk to it from Slack. Either way it's one real, virtual machine for your team to compound together.

Building this deep into infrastructure was a radical move for us. It's kind of hilarious that we now find ourselves in the virtual machine business. But AI has changed the dynamics of what's possible. I actually think the right approach with AI is to be 100x more ambitious than you otherwise would be.

## Rise of the 100x Marketer

We keep seeing AI launch videos that say the marketer is cooked. We could not disagree more.

AI is an amplifier. If you are an elite marketer like @HireFireTeam you will get unbelievable leverage. But if you outsource your judgment to AI, you'll build a token guzzling slop cannon.

The best marketers know when to be rigorous with data and when to ignore it. They know creative is everything, but that the goal is revenue not art.

They jump altitudes in a heartbeat. One moment they are writing direct-response hooks, the next moment they are jamming on brand narrative with the board. The best of the best know those are the same thing.

The best marketers run on a system. It's often swirling around in their mind or at best locked away in a Mac Mini. We didn't build Runneth to replace them. We built it to make them a force of nature, and to lift everyone around them.

AI can be chaotic. Runneth exists to keep the best marketers calm and in control while they work at a scale that feels impossible.
