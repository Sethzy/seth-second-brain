## Verbatim Text

openclaw + arcads = the new content stack

i went from a topic idea to a fully produced 5-minute video in under 2 hours.

openclaw researched the topic, found the angles, and wrote the script. arcads turned that script into a full talking-head video with an AI presenter.

here's exactly how to build it:

old way: you have an idea -> you research manually -> you write a script -> you set up a filming day -> you shoot -> you edit for hours -> you finally publish (if you don't abandon it halfway through)

new way: you have an idea -> you get openclaw to research and write the script -> you then take the script into arcads which produces the video -> you publish

layer 1: openclaw (the research + script engine)

I won't go into actually setting up openclaw in this article as there are tons of resources out there already, so I’m assuming that you already have that set up.

once you do, here’s what to do next:

start by having openclaw scrape the landscape first. ask it to "find the top 10 videos on [your topic]. analyze their hooks, structure, and angles. what's working? what's missing?"

then, start mining voice of customers. "scrape reddit threads, amazon reviews, and forum posts about [topic]. what language do real people use? what questions keep coming up? what frustrations do they have?"

next you begin looking for the gap. "based on this research, what angle isn't being covered? what would make someone click this over everything else that exists?"

lastly, you generate the script. "write an 8-minute video script on [your angle]. use the customer language we found. structure it with a hook, 4 main sections, and a cta. pace it for speaking."

you now have a research-backed script that's ready to produce.

layer 2: arcads (the production engine)

arcads just dropped unlimited-length video generation. this is the piece that makes the whole system work.

here's how to use it:

go to talking actor -> select "audio-driven" or "omnihuman" as the model

then for actual production, you have 2 options:

option 1 — script-to-video:

paste your script (no character limit now). pick your avatar. choose a voice. hit generate. Done.

option 2 — speech-to-speech:

record yourself reading the script with your natural pacing and pauses. upload the audio. the AI avatar mirrors your exact delivery - your rhythm, your emphasis, your personality. just not your face.

imo speech-to-speech is the move if you want human-feeling content without being on camera. you simply talk and the AI presents.

for long scripts, the system automatically splits into ~20-second segments for quality, then stitches them together. this is how you get consistent character throughout.

here are different ways I see this being leveraged:

- youtube channel: research trending topics weekly. generate scripts. produce videos. publish consistently without production days eating your calendar.

- course content: outline 10 modules. research and script each one. produce all 10 videos. entire course done in a week instead of a month.

- content repurposing: one deep research session → one 20-minute video → chop into shorts, clips, tweets, linkedin posts. one input, entire content ecosystem.

- client content: research their industry. script videos in their niche. produce at scale. offer video content as a service without a production team.

some tips for best results:

- spend more time on the research prompt than the script prompt. garbage research = garbage script. quality research = script that writes itself.

- use speech-to-speech if tone matters. text-to-video is faster but speech-to-speech captures your actual delivery style.

- for long videos (10+ min), break your script into clear sections. helps with pacing and makes the final output feel more structured.

- use a high-quality avatar image as your starting frame. consistency depends on it.

the bottleneck was never ideas. it was production. this stack removes it.

i'm building more systems like this every week. follow if you want them as they drop @VibeMarketer_
