The Truth About AI Slop on TikTok

## Is it actually a money printing machine? Is it as easy as people make it out to be?

## Is the FOMO real?

## If you've asked yourself some of these questions over the past few weeks seeing all of the AI UGC slop, then this article is for you.

---

## tl;dr of how you can do it the easiest way:

- Research your TA. Create a bunch of narratives. Get the angles right. Tailor the prompts. Craft coherent and *relevant* storylines.

- Set up every account properly. Do not get them flagged. If you do, burn it and create a new one. Brainrot for a while. Teach the algo you're a human.

- Measure everything. Obsess over numbers. Track performance. Gather numerical feedback and understand what it practically implies before moving forward.

Foundations.

Automation is a volume game. The higher your throughput, the higher the odds of catching the wave and going viral. While the mainstream approach is mostly optimizing replication for production capacity, the actual creative work still demands human manual effort. This, for now, is the only way to end up among the significant few in the Pareto distribution. Meaning, among the people who rack up dozens of millions of views on TikTok with AI UGC.

This article is split into three acts. Narrative choice, account set up, and iterating on feedback. Each one is based on my personal experience farming attention on TikTok with slideshows (although everything here applies to AI video creators too).

---

# Act 1. The Narrative.

Good storytelling can single-handedly make or break your outcome. Properly engineering the whole storyline and manually handpicking hooks to lead the narrative will get your engagement from graph #1 (bad hook), graph #2 (not interesting), or graph #3 (no value) to graph #4 (banger video).

The niche matters less than the story. Find what your audience already cares about and fit the product into that world. Trading app? Easy wins and the lifestyle that follows. Fitness app? Jacked boys and girls pumping the weights and flexing muscles. Meal prep app? Merge the food and fitness angles. The story carries more weight on TikTok. And showcasing the product is part of that.

It's worth spending the majority of efforts to nail this part down. The research, the language, the writing, the insights, and then overall positioning. All of that. And graph #4 is a living example of what the outcome will look like. People don't drop off at crazy rates, the video itself is well put together, enough to keep viewers' attention. Paired with a good storyline culminating at the end with a clear CTA is destined to become a decent CTR.

Retention rates can tell you what needs a fix. It's usually either wrong TA for the narrative, or the quality of your video, unclear CTA, etc. Look into it and decide what is lagging. Also, make sure you are using a recognizable viral song that goes with your CTA clip.

A perfect narrative running on a flagged profile will not get anywhere. So next comes creating accounts and seeding the narratives.

# Act 2. Setting Up Your Accounts.

98% of the traffic at first will come from 'For you' section. It's TikTok algorithm pushing your videos to random people. Geography of the viewers is heavily dependent on where your account is registered. Which consequently influences the revenue. Markets with higher GDP convert better, on average.

So properly setting up the foundation for the farm comes second after nailing down the narrative.

Personally I lost 5/8 accounts that I registered. Once the algo flaggs the profile as automated or suspicious, the videos will not get recommended and shown to people. The account is thrown into a zero views chamber which is extremely difficult to break out of. Burn the account, sacrifice it to the greater cause, and create a new one.

What to pay attention to, based on my own empirical data (or what I learned FAFO'ing):

1. Register some new accounts, one per device. Use a proxy if targeting a region outside of your device location, set the phone to that region, switch the app store to that region, and download TikTok with the proxy on. Do not turn the proxy off when TikTok is running on the background. And preferably use the same WiFi network every time you open the app.

2. Spend at least 2 weeks brainrotting on TikTok, interacting with accounts in your niche, liking, sharing, and commenting. Upload one video per day max during this period.

3. I would refrain from connecting any automation tools to accounts for a while. Do it once you enter the scaling stage.

Additional tips:

- Don't use the same network connection for many accounts. TikTok algo spots same IPs.

- Use anti detect browser tooling.

- And combine it with a good proxi.

# Act 3. Measure, Feedback, Scale.

Grind pays off in the form of total views to engagement ratio (if applicable) and click through rates (the CTA).

At this stage seed the narratives and see which ones resonate with your TA. It's going to take some time though. Be ready to dedicate a few weeks to it. Unless you're really smart or lucky.

Different narratives hit differently. I got 5x-6x variation in view count, video per video, across newly created accounts. Even if the difference is at 10, 100, 500, or 1000 views mark at this stage, it is still worth paying attention to. Doubling down on volume for each locally winning narrative can compound into 10x total views over time. And if you have the bandwidth to creatively wrap the winning compound and present it better than anyone in your niche, or approach it from a completely different angle, you can start competing on another level and establish something close to a traffic monopoly. Peter Thiel style.

My bet is mostly on girl AI UGC because it's the hottest and, let's be real, the most attention-capturing meta. And it has been for the longest time...

Once all accounts are warm and running, churning through 100 to 2000 views per post, start posting 2 to 3 videos per day per account. See what was working better than anything else during the warm up stage and double down on it over the next week. If nothing goes viral, meaning at least 10x your average views, try new formats and see what works.

Now, if there's a viral post that landed well, how to convert all that traffic to user signups? AS far as I know there's only three ways to capture part of that traffic:

1. CTA + link in the profile.

2. CTA + link in the comments.

3. CTA + link in the video.
