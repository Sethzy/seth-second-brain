## Verbatim Text

8 MCPs that EVERY modern sales team should connect to: 

1. Apollo

Apollo handles prospect research at the depth a contact list alone never reaches.

Beyond list-building, Apollo identifies multiple decision-makers across a target account, organized by seniority.

A single-threaded outbound play loses the second another decision-maker joins the call.

Apollo's MCP turns a contact pull into a stakeholder map in one prompt.

2. Findymail

Apollo gets you most of the way there. Findymail closes the gap.

For every contact Apollo can't locate an email for, Findymail runs a waterfall verification.

For a list of 500 LinkedIn URLs with no emails attached, Findymail batch-verifies before any of them enter Instantly.

The sequence runs against verified contacts only.

The domain stays warm and the replies actually land in the primary inbox.

3. Firecrawl

Firecrawl gives Claude access to the open web.

• Competitor pricing pages, scraped on demand.
• Hiring signals from a target account's careers tab.
• A prospect's recent blog posts, read for personalization.

Apollo gets you the contact. Findymail verifies the address. Firecrawl reads the world around them.

Those 3 tools complete the data layer.

4. Instantly

Instantly is the email sequencer.

The MCP turns the dashboard work into a conversation with Claude.

"Build a 4-step campaign for the RevOps leaders we just sourced. Step 1 references their recent funding round. Step 2 shares a relevant case study. Step 3 is a soft pitch. Step 4 is a breakup."

Claude writes the copy, picks warmed inboxes, uploads leads with custom fields, and schedules sending.

The sequence is LIVE before the next standup.

5. HeyReach

HeyReach is the LinkedIn-side counterpart to Instantly.

It manages automated DM campaigns across multiple sender accounts at once.

Paired with a signal tool like Trigify, the play looks like this:

• Trigify watches a CEO's LinkedIn posts.
• An ICP-matched account engages on Monday.
• HeyReach sends a personalized connection request and runs a DM sequence.
• The full play lands by Friday with the engagement context attached.

LinkedIn engagement turns into a routed CRM event in 5 days.

6. Gmail

The Gmail MCP is a search-and-summarize layer over your inbox.

Before a discovery call, Claude reads every email thread you've ever had with the contact and returns a paragraph summarizing the deal history.

The brief lands in your prep doc in 30 seconds.

Every rep walks into every call with the full deal history loaded.

7. Slack

Slack is where internal handoffs and customer questions actually live.

The Slack MCP pulls customer conversation history into a Claude session, drafts DM replies in the right voice, and routes inbound signals to the right rep with full context attached.

The customer history that used to live across channels now lives one prompt away.

8. HubSpot

HubSpot is where everything lands.

The MCP lets Claude update contacts, companies, and deals; fill CRM data from Apollo, Findymail, and Firecrawl as the data flows in; and answer pipeline questions without anyone opening a dashboard.

"Which deals are stuck in stage 3?"

Claude reads HubSpot, returns the list, and pulls the email history for each one from Gmail in the same response.

Salesforce offers comparable functionality through its own MCP.

TL;DR: 

1. Apollo gets you the list.
2. Findymail verifies the address.
3. Firecrawl reads the open web.
4. Instantly runs the email sequence.
5. HeyReach runs the LinkedIn sequence.
6. Gmail loads the deal history.
7. Slack routes the internal context.
8. HubSpot is the system of record.
