Vibe coding is HOT.

It quietly wipes out 80+ hours of manual RevOps work every month. But most people are thinking about it the wrong way…

There’s a massive opportunity for GTM & Ops leaders to change how they operate.

The general narrative around vibe coding platforms is that they’re meant for coders and entrepreneurs to kick off new businesses…

At least that’s how I interpreted it.

BUT after digging deeper, I came across a ton of use cases to vibe code your own internal solutions, the ones that remove the painful, repetitive, 80-hour problems your team keeps dealing with every month.

Here are 3 examples:

--> Territory planning & account router

Apply your own custom rules (industry, ARR band, geo, partner involvement)
to generate territory splits and account assignments automatically.

--> Lead routing & SLA monitoring

Build a small app that connects to your CRM via API and: 

-Applies your routing logic (segment, intent, previous owner)
-Flags SLA risks in real time and alerts in Slack
-Produces a live dashboard with time-to-first-touch, SLA breach counts, and chronic slow responders

--> Pipeline hygiene & forecast assistant

-Pulls open opportunities
-Flags issues (no next step, stale stages, pushed close dates, missing contacts)
-Uses your rules + LLM scoring to highlight risk

Do I believe you can “vibe code” a new BI tool, CRM, or forecasting platform?

No.

But for targeted use cases, especially for orgs without unlimited budgets and insane complexity... it’s a fantastic way to eliminate massive manual effort.
