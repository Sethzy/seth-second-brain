## 5 Agentic AI Workflows Saving Me 10+ Hours Every Week

1. Podcast Production Agent (Saves 4 hours/week)

What it does:

After I finish recording a podcast episode, my agent handles the entire post-production workflow. Transcribes the audio, writes show notes in my voice, generates 3 LinkedIn post graphics, drafts the guest thank-you email, and creates YouTube description copy.

How it works:

I drop the audio file in a folder. The agent detects it, triggers the workflow, and delivers everything to my Discord in 15 minutes. I review, approve, publish.

My time: 15 minutes (used to be 4+ hours)

Tools: @openclaw with custom skills (transcription, brand-voice, social-graphics, email-drafting)

---

2. Email Triage & Response Agent (Saves 3 hours/week)

What it does:

Reads my inbox, categorizes emails (urgent, client, sales, spam), drafts responses for 80% of messages, and flags the 20% that need my personal attention.

How it works:

Every morning at 6am, the agent scans my inbox. It drafts replies using my voice profile, saves them as drafts, and sends me a summary: "12 emails processed. 9 drafts ready for review. 3 need your input."

My time: 30 minutes to review drafts (used to be 3.5 hours responding manually)

Tools: @openclaw Gmail integration + brand-voice skill

---

3. Content Repurposing Agent (Saves 2 hours/week)

What it does:

Takes my weekly YouTube video and automatically creates: LinkedIn post, X thread, Instagram caption, email newsletter section, and blog post outline.

How it works:

I publish a YouTube video. Agent pulls the transcript, analyzes key points, and writes platform-specific content in my voice. Delivers all 5 formats in one batch.

My time: 20 minutes to edit and post (used to be 2+ hours writing from scratch)

Tools: YouTube transcript API + @openclaw with platform-specific skills

---

4. Research & Briefing Agent (Saves 1.5 hours/week)

What it does:

Before podcast interviews, the agent researches my guest (finds their best content, extracts key themes, identifies unique angles, and creates a briefing doc with suggested questions).

How it works:

I add the guest's name and LinkedIn URL to a spreadsheet. Agent searches their recent posts, podcast appearances, and interviews. Synthesizes everything into a 2-page brief with 10 question suggestions.

My time: 10 minutes to review (used to be 90+ minutes manual research)

Tools: Brave Search API + web scraping + Claude synthesis

---

5. Calendar & Meeting Prep Agent (Saves 1 hour/week)

What it does:

Checks my calendar every morning, pulls context for each meeting (previous emails, project notes, last conversation summary), and creates a 1-page prep sheet for my day.

How it works:

At 5am, agent scans my Google Calendar. For each meeting, it searches my email, Notion workspace, and previous meeting notes. Generates a briefing: "10am - Client call with Sarah. Last discussed: AI assessment scope. Action items from last call: [3 items]. Today's agenda: [suggested topics]."

My time: 5 minutes to read (used to be 60+ minutes scrambling before calls)

Tools: @openclaw + Gmail/Calendar search + Notion integration

## The Pattern You Should Notice

All 5 workflows share 3 things:

1. They handle tasks I do every week (not one-off projects)

2. They follow a predictable pattern (perfect for automation)

3. They require judgment, not just data entry (that's where AI shines)

The difference between "AI tools" and "agentic AI" is simple:

AI tools wait for you to ask. Agentic AI takes action when conditions are met.

Podcast file uploaded? Agent runs production workflow.

Email received? Agent drafts response.

Meeting scheduled? Agent prepares briefing.

No prompting. No manual triggers. It just happens.

## How to Build Your Own Agentic Workflows

Start here:

Step 1: List tasks you do 3+ times per week

Step 2: Pick one that takes 20+ minutes each time

Step 3: Map the steps (what do you actually do, in order?)

Step 4: Identify which steps require human judgment vs. execution

Step 5: Build the execution steps as an agent workflow

Most people try to automate everything at once. That's why they fail.

Start with one workflow. Get it working. Then add the next one.

I didn't build all 5 of these in a week. I built them over 3 months. One at a time. Each one proved the concept, so the next one was easier.

The ROI is real. 12 hours returned per week = 624 hours per year.

That's 15 work weeks. I didn't hire anyone. I just taught my agent what to do when certain conditions are met.

That's agentic AI.
