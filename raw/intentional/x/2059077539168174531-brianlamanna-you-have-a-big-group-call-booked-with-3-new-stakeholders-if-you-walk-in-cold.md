You have a big group call booked with 3 new stakeholders. 

If you walk in cold, you're starting from zero with people who have no reason to trust you yet.

A quick warm-up note 2-3 days before a multi-stakeholder meeting is one of the most underused plays. 

It builds goodwill, shapes the agenda, and gets them pre-sold before you even show up. 

𝗛𝗲𝗿𝗲'𝘀 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝘁𝗲𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗮𝘁𝗲:

Hey {first name},

Heard great things about you from working with {champion's name} and excited to get your perspective on {your company} for {their title} this {date}!

We plan to highlight {goal of call}. Anything else you want to ensure we work into the agenda?

Highly recommend you check this out too:
- {Digital sales room, case study, previous demo, etc.}

Excited to meet! 

{Your name}

Two things this accomplishes simultaneously: 
1. The new stakeholder feels seen before the call even starts
2. You get a mini-discovery moment that could completely reshape your agenda.

That "anything else" question has surfaced objections I wouldn't have known until they blew up live.
 
Send this every single time without exception.
