"why should I use @openclaw?" 

Here are the most useful use cases, from my experience setting up my own bot (@avareed_1994) 

(i) One off asks:

- buy something for me: this morning I realized the hydrogen peroxide bottle is running out, so I asked Ava to add it to my Amazon cart
- specific deep research tasks: I'm not going to Chatgpt anymore to run deep research, Ava handles that for me. I have 1-2 requests like this per day, that now Ava handles completely. Usually I’m in a meeting and ask her to pull more context and email it to us (it's insane how fast this happens - usually within 2 mins)
- vibe-coding a report/landing page: I'm usually asking Ava to create a lot of interfaces for things that i want to present to the team or visualize (using Vercel as a skill for this)
- write content; I share Google docs with early thoughts or voice memos and have Ava finish my posts. She already has gotten better in understanding my writing style. The articles that i get from her are orders of magnitude better than just an one-off request with ChatGPT
- set calendar invites: I was sitting with a friend over coffee and I sent a voice note to Ava to create an event in my calendar, which she did flawlessly
- builds quick functional prototypes: Yesterday we had a cool idea for a growth tool at work, so I gave Ava the details and asked her to spin up a prototype for us to review and iterate on - she is activelly working with me and my team to specify and build any of the ideas we have

(ii) Proactive tasks (cron jobs)

- daily briefing; Ava gives me her daily plan of what she plans to do today, and she asks me if there are blockers or questions for me
- daily health reports; she has my wearable data (apple watch/oura ring) and gives me a report of my day right in chat
- calendar/email assistance; Ava looks at my calendar every day, and asks if I need help to prep (especially for meetings where I'm the organizer)
- plans my travel for me and vibe-codes a web app that I can share with my sister; Ava is currently activelly tracking price changes for certain flights and hotels over the next month to create the best itinerary for our Yosemite trip this autumn
- run a weekly scan over my competitor twitter activities; Ava compares our activities with our competitors and sends me a comparison report of what they've done better this week
- remind me to do work that I never get to do: I eventually want to write a book, and I've asked Ava to give me a writing prompt every Friday and keep me accountable to my goal
- grow your socials: I have Ava analyzing my twitter activity and suggest new posts for me (I have 1k+ followers since I started doing that)

--

Have in mind that most of these use-cases are when Ava doesn't have access to my work apps (yet). And yes, this is definitely possible via other platforms/tools/apps/code etc, but I like this more because:

- It's right there in my Telegram chat
- It's persistent, it can actually remember stuff for me
- It's proactive 
- I use it way more often, because my phone is always with me and I don’t need to build anything to get an answer
- I don't have to build any infra/logic and Ava handles that for me
