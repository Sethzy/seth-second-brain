## Verbatim Text

The latest model releases are a good reminder to Stop asking which model is the smartest. Start asking how much your agents are burning while you sleep.

The era of $20/month flat-rate AI is dead for enterprise builders. Salesforce charging $2 a conversation, Zendesk moving to pay-on-resolution—these aren't blips. They're the new economics of agentic workflows.

Here’s the reality nobody wants to put on a vendor slide: Agentic loops consume roughly 15x more compute than traditional chat interactions. One developer running a background agent can generate the infrastructure load of 20 people. 

When AI transitions from answering questions to executing autonomous loops, the traditional SaaS spending model fundamentally breaks.

If you deploy agents without an embedded control plane, you're essentially handing an uncapped corporate card to an algorithm that doesn't sleep.

The new non-negotiable for enterprise AI isn't a better model leaderboard score. It’s cost governance and per-run ledgers.

To survive the shift to an agentic operating model, you need to know: 1. Which specific agent identity initiated the run 2. What tools, sandbox environments, and external data it touched 3. The exact token cost of that specific workflow—in real-time

This is why the smartest buyers are pivoting aggressively toward the "Agent Control Plane." They are prioritizing deterministic runtime policy, auditable state, and explicit cost-tracking over raw model novelty.

If you can't tie an agent's compute spend to a specific, measurable workflow outcome, you aren't doing AI transformation. You're just blindly subsidizing hyperscaler capex.

Are your AI workflows governed by a control plane, or are you just paying the toll?
