AI Search Decoded — Day 2 of 10

Yesterday I explained how AI search is a decision engine, not a search engine. Today: the three acronyms everyone is confused about.

SEO. AEO. GEO. What they actually mean and how they're connected.

Let's use one example throughout. You sell a protein powder.

SEO — Search Engine Optimization
You already know this one. Someone types "best protein powder" into Google. Your job is to rank on page one. Keywords, backlinks, page speed, content structure, domain authority.

The goal: get the click from a list of 10 blue links.
SEO is alive. Google still processes billions of searches.

But here's the shift: over 60% of Google searches now end without a click to any website. AI Overviews answer the question before anyone scrolls down.

SEO gets you indexed. That still matters. But indexed doesn't mean recommended anymore.

AEO — Answer Engine Optimization
Someone asks Google "what's the best protein powder for muscle recovery?" Google shows an AI Overview at the top. A direct answer. No clicking required.

AEO is about structuring your content so AI can extract it as that direct answer. Featured snippets. People Also Ask boxes. Voice assistant responses. AI Overviews.

Your protein powder page needs clear, structured answers to common questions. Not marketing copy. 

Direct answers. "This protein powder contains 25g whey isolate per serving, is third-party tested, and free from soy and gluten."

The goal: be the answer AI extracts, not just a link it lists.

GEO — Generative Engine Optimization
Someone asks ChatGPT "recommend a protein powder that's third-party tested, no soy, good for recovery, under $40."

ChatGPT doesn't show your website. It recommends 3 to 5 specific products by name. With reasoning. With sources. Your brand is either in that response or it doesn't exist for that buyer.

GEO is about making your brand the one AI cites and recommends across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and AI Overviews.

The goal: be recommended by name when AI answers a buying question.

How they connect
Think of it as layers:

SEO gets you indexed and ranked on Google. That's the foundation. Without it, AI can't find you.

AEO gets you extracted as a direct answer in Google's AI features. You're not just ranked. You're the answer.

GEO gets you recommended by name across all AI engines. Not just Google. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude. All of them.

Each layer builds on the one before. Skip SEO and AI can't find your content. Skip AEO and Google's AI features ignore you. Skip GEO and every AI engine outside Google doesn't know you exist.

Google just confirmed this last week.
They released an official guide saying AEO and GEO are not separate disciplines. They're SEO evolved. Same index. Same quality signals. Same ranking systems. But the way users interact with results has changed fundamentally.

The brands that treat these as three separate budgets are overcomplicating it. The brands that treat them as one connected strategy with different outputs will win.

The uncomfortable number

ChatGPT processes 2.5 billion prompts daily. 65% qualify as search queries. But ChatGPT has 96% lower click-through rate than Google.

Your brand can be mentioned by ChatGPT to millions of people and your analytics dashboard shows nothing. No traffic. No click. No attribution. But the buyer just decided based on that recommendation.

That's why this matters. The influence is massive. The measurement is broken. The brands that understand this early will build visibility while everyone else is still arguing about whether GEO is real.
