## Verbatim Text

The hardest skill to teach a newer rep isn't prospecting or discovery.

It's disqualification.

Not because the red flags are invisible. It's because they don’t have enough reps under their belt to have that ‘gut feel.’

No single flag feels like enough. So they keep going.

𝗛𝗲𝗿𝗲'𝘀 𝘄𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗜 𝘄𝗮𝘁𝗰𝗵 𝗳𝗼𝗿...

→ The champion won't loop in others. They keep saying "let me share this internally" but never do. That's more of a dead end wearing a name tag than a champion.

→ The business case is surface level. "We want to improve coaching." Okay. Coaching of what? Toward what outcome? Who owns that number? If they can't answer, there's no urgency.

→ The tech stack is a problem. For Gong specifically - we need to be able to connect to their web meetings, phone systems, and CRM. Workarounds are possible but not ideal.

→ The company has never invested in anything like what you sell. They go with the cheapest option every time. In our world, it’d be like them choosing Google Sheets to keep track of deals instead of Saleesforce.

→ Constant meeting reschedules. The next step keeps getting pushed. Three weeks becomes five. Five becomes "let's reconnect next quarter."

None of these alone kills a deal.

But when they start stacking, you have to pay attention.

Disqualify early. Protect your pipeline. And time.
