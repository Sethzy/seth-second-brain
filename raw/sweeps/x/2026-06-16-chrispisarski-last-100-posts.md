how to handle the most common sales objections:

1) "send me an email with more info"
=> "of course, I would love to. Is there anything you would want to see in that email?”

2) "your price is too high"
=> "help me understand - too high compared to what exactly?"

prospects say this even when they're planning to buy. they want to see if you'll fold. don't. make them justify the comparison first.

3) "we tried something like this before and it didn't work"
=> "that's really useful to know. what specifically went wrong - was it the product, the rollout, or the team that owned it?"

4) "i need to run this by my boss first"
=> "makes sense. what's the one question you think your boss is going to push back on the hardest?"

if you let them sell it for you, you'll lose. get in the room or at least coach them on exactly what to say

5) "the timing just isn't right"
=> "I hear you. is it timing because something else is taking priority right now, or is it more that this problem isn't urgent enough to act on yet?"

"bad timing" means usually = "not a big enough priority." find out which one it is before you follow up

6) "your tool doesn't integrate with our stack"
=> "what's the integration that's most critical for you?"

most of the time it's one thing. and most of the time you either have it or can build it. don't let a concern take a deal without digging into what they actually need

7) "we're still evaluating other options"
=> "totally fair. without naming anyone, are you comparing us on price, on features, or on something else?"

if you don't know what you're being compared on, you can't win. get them to tell you
